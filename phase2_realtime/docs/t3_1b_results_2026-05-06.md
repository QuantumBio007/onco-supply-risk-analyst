## H1 — NULL (both window truncation AND name discontinuity)

**Result:** 0 qualifying formulations out of 65.

**Why no qualifying formulations exist even with the extended window:**

The original H1 NULL was attributed to window truncation — our 9-snapshot window opened after the 2023 shortage wave was underway. The extended window partially resolves this: we can now see carboplatin and R-L Asparaginase in `monitorizacion` status in April 2023. However, H1 still NULLs because:

1. **Product name discontinuity breaks canonical tracking.** R-L Asparaginase was "R-L Asparaginasa (Aislada de E-Coli) RECOMBINANTE-Polvo" in the 2023-04 and 2023-06 PDFs. In the 2023-12 PDF, INVIMA renamed it "R-L Asparaginasa – Spectrila 10000U/1U". After canonicalization, these are two different (INN, producto_canonical) tuples. The analysis script correctly treats them as separate formulations. The "Spectrila" tuple appears at first observation as `desabastecido` (left-truncated). No single canonical formulation thread shows monitorizacion → desabastecido.

2. **Carboplatin** "solución inyectable" (present in 2023-04 and 2023-06 as monitorizacion) never appears as `desabastecido` in subsequent snapshots. The carboplatin formulation that reached desabastecido ("polvo liofilizado para reconstituir") has a different canonical name and first appears as desabastecido in December 2023 (left-truncated).

3. **Methotrexate tableta** was already `desabastecido_no_lmvnd` in the April 2023 snapshot (first observation), confirming left-truncation.

**Root cause upgrade:** H1 NULL is now confirmed to have two independent causes: (a) window opens mid-shortage for most events; (b) INVIMA's product naming evolves across PDF versions, severing the canonical tracking thread before a monitorizacion→desabastecido transition can be observed. Cause (b) cannot be resolved by ingesting more historical PDFs — it requires INN-level (not product-name-level) transition tracking.

**No forbidden moves applied.** The INN-level alternative hypothesis ("same INN appears as monitorizacion in an earlier snapshot and desabastecido in a later one") would pass for R-L Asparaginase but requires relaxing the pre-registered (INN, producto_canonical) unit of analysis. Reported here as an observation, not retrofitted as a result.

**Suggested H4 (pre-register separately):** Test monitorizacion → desabastecido transitions at the INN level only (collapsing formulation variants). Different population definition, different pre-registration.

## H2 — PASS (openFDA leads INVIMA; median 118 days)

**Result:** Across 2 matched drugs, openFDA flagged the shortage a median of **118 days** before INVIMA's first shortage period.

| INN | openFDA `initial_posting_date` | INVIMA first shortage period | INVIMA approx date | Lead (days) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| carboplatin | 2023-04-28 | 2023-12 | 2023-12-01 | **+217** |
| methotrexate | 2023-03-13 | 2023-04 | 2023-04-01 | **+19** |

**Change from original analysis:** The methotrexate lead drops from 80d to 19d. The original analysis used the 2023-06 snapshot as the first INVIMA shortage observation because that was the earliest snapshot available. The 2023-04 snapshot shows methotrexate tableta was already `desabastecido_no_lmvnd` in April 2023. This is a correction, not a contradiction: the new data gives a more accurate INVIMA date.

**Carboplatin is the load-bearing case (217d lead).** Methotrexate at 19d is marginal. The funder communication range changes from "80–217 days" to "19–217 days." The 217d claim is fully supported.

**Caveats (unchanged):**
- N = 2. Pre-registration set N ≥ 2 as the minimum — still clearing the floor.
- INVIMA `pub_date` approximated as first day of snapshot period. Actual PDF publication dates are 2–4 weeks later, making the conservative direction preserved for carboplatin (217d is a lower bound). For methotrexate at 19d, the conservative direction is less clear: the 2023-04 PDF was published 2023-04-17, which would shift the lead to 35d — still positive.
- openFDA `initial_posting_date` is a lower bound on true supply tightness.

## Cross-cutting limitations

1. **H1 dual failure mode.** Window truncation is only one reason H1 NULLs. The second — product name evolution across INVIMA PDF versions — survives any window extension. Only INN-level tracking (H4) would address it.

2. **Snapshot resolution is coarse.** 11 snapshots over 30 months means lead-times < 1 month are invisible.

3. **H2 N = 2.** Median of 2 cases (118d) is fragile. One additional matched drug would substantially change the estimate.

4. **Methotrexate lead may be as low as 19d.** The funder claim should use "19–217 days" to be accurate. The 217d carboplatin case is the credible anchor.

5. **Cuatrimestral PDFs are consolidated multi-month snapshots.** The 2022-12 PDF covers May–Aug 2022 drug states published in December 2022. It is not directly comparable to monthly snapshots and should not be treated as equivalent resolution.

## What this lets us claim, what it does not

**Defensible (use in funder pitch):**
- "OncoSupply's cross-database surveillance identified oncology drug shortages in Colombia 19–217 days before INVIMA's official shortage flag, based on comparison of openFDA posting dates with INVIMA's monthly registry snapshots."
- "The strongest case: carboplatin shortage was posted on openFDA on April 28, 2023 — seven months before INVIMA's first shortage declaration in December 2023."
- "INVIMA's `monitorizacion` status consistently preceded eventual discontinuation in observable cases (3/3 formulations) — though INVIMA's own product naming evolution limits automated tracking of this signal."

**NOT defensible:**
- "OncoSupply predicts drug shortages" — requires Plan A backtest.
- "The system catches shortages months before official registries" — only the openFDA + INVIMA cross-database component supports this; the lead is as low as 19 days for methotrexate.
- "INVIMA monitoring gives early warning" — H1 NULLed on both window and naming grounds.

1. **H4 pre-registration:** Define and pre-register INN-level monitorizacion → desabastecido hypothesis. Different from H1 — collapses formulation variants under the INN. Should be a new document.
