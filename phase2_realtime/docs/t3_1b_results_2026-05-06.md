## H1 — NULL (insufficient observable transitions)

**Result:** 0 qualifying formulations out of 57. Pre-registration required N ≥ 3 for any verdict other than NULL.

**Why no qualifying formulations existed:**
- Of 22 oncology INNs in our window, 5 formulations ever reached `desabastecido*` status.
- All 5 were **left-truncated** — they appeared with `desabastecido*` in their FIRST observation snapshot, meaning the shortage was declared before our window opened.
- Specifically: asparaginase (3 formulations), methotrexate tableta, carboplatin polvo liofilizado para reconstituir.
- **No formulation in our window transitioned monitorizacion → desabastecido**. The transitions present in the data are different shapes: monitorizacion → descontinuado (skipping desabastecido), or already-desabastecido → descontinuado.

**Honest reading:** Our 9-snapshot window (28 calendar months, 2023-06 onward) opened DURING or AFTER the 2023 LATAM oncology shortage event peak. The leading-indicator signal we hypothesized — `monitorizacion` preceding `desabastecido` — would have been observable in 2022 / early-2023 INVIMA reports that we have not ingested.

**No forbidden moves applied.** The available tighter hypothesis ("monitorizacion → descontinuado lead-time") would be a post-hoc reformulation, declined per pre-registration discipline. Reported as suggested H4 for future work.

**Implication for OncoSupply:** The lead-time signal H1 hypothesized cannot be tested with current data. To resolve, ingest INVIMA PDFs from 2022-01 → 2023-05 (estimated 1-2 days of work — same parser, additional historical PDFs).

## H2 — PASS (openFDA leads INVIMA by 148.5 days median)

**Result:** Across 2 matched drugs (drugs in both databases that reached INVIMA shortage status), openFDA flagged the shortage a median of **148.5 days** before INVIMA.

| INN | openFDA `initial_posting_date` | INVIMA first shortage period | INVIMA approx date | Lead (days) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| carboplatin | 2023-04-28 | 2023-12 | 2023-12-01 | **+217** |
| methotrexate | 2023-03-13 | 2023-06 | 2023-06-01 | **+80** |

**No forbidden moves applied.** Bleomycin matched on INN (openFDA initial 2025-06-13) but did not reach INVIMA shortage status in our window — correctly excluded per pre-registration.

**Caveats explicitly noted:**
- N = 2 is small. The pre-registration set N ≥ 2 as the minimum admissible — this is the floor, not strong power.
- INVIMA `pub_date` approximated by first day of snapshot period. Actual PDF publication dates differ (typically 2-4 weeks later). Adjusting for this would INCREASE lead-days, not decrease — the conservative direction is preserved.
- openFDA `initial_posting_date` is the date the FDA shortage registry first received the entry. Real-world supply tightness predates this. The reported lead-days are a lower bound on the true supply-signal lead.

**This is the strongest finding of the day.** It validates a specific, defensible OncoSupply value claim: monitoring openFDA's English-language shortage registry provides 80–217 days of lead-time on official LATAM (Colombia) shortage declarations. A registry monitor that combines openFDA + INVIMA + Spanish-language news is a leading indicator by construction.

**Implication for OncoSupply:** This is the result to put in the funder pitch. Frame it as "cross-database surveillance gives 3–7 months of leading signal," not as "the news pipeline predicts shortages."

## H3 — PASS (borderline; left-truncated count equals observable count)

**Result:** 100% of 2 observable `descontinuado` formulations had a prior `monitorizacion` or `desabastecido*` snapshot before reaching descontinuado. Pre-registered threshold: ≥ 80%.

| Formulation | First obs period | First obs estado | First descontinuado period |
|---|---|---|---|
| carboplatin polvo liofilizado para reconstituir | 2023-12 | desabastecido | 2024-12 |
| carboplatin polvo liofilizado solución inyectable vial | 2023-12 | monitorizacion | 2024-04 |

**Left-truncated formulations (already descontinuado at first observation):**
- cisplatin solución inyectable (descontinuado 2023-12, first observed 2023-12)
- tamoxifen tableta (descontinuado 2023-06, first observed 2023-06; canonicalization conflated 10 mg + 20 mg variants)

**Verdict mechanics:** Pre-registration specified PARTIAL-PASS if `left_truncated > observable`. Here `left_truncated = 2` and `observable = 2`, so strict-greater fails and PASS holds. **One additional left-truncated case would have flipped this to PARTIAL-PASS.** This is borderline and should be communicated as such.

**No forbidden moves applied.**

**Caveats:**
- N = 4 total descontinuado formulations is small.
- The carboplatin "para reconstituir" case is observable per H3 definition (first estado = `desabastecido`, not `descontinuado`) but was H1-left-truncated (already in shortage). H3 is therefore largely tracking "did the carboplatin transition from desabastecido to descontinuado leave a trail" — yes, with ~12 months of intermediate observations.
- The tamoxifen case is contaminated by canonicalization (dose conflation). Excluding it strengthens the H3 verdict, but excluding cases post-hoc is a forbidden move.

**Implication for OncoSupply:** H3 is consistent with the claim that registry-monitoring captures impending discontinuations, but the sample is too small and too left-truncated for this to be a load-bearing finding. Treat as supporting evidence for H2, not a standalone result.

## Cross-cutting limitations (mandatory reporting)

1. **Window left-truncation dominates.** Of every "interesting" event in our 22 oncology INNs, the majority occurred at or before our first snapshot (2023-06). Our data captures the *aftermath* of the 2023 platinum-chemotherapy shortage triggered by Intas Pharmaceuticals (India) FDA shutdown in November 2022, not its onset. To capture leading indicators, we need INVIMA PDFs from 2022.

2. **Snapshot resolution is coarse.** 9 snapshots over 28 months means our smallest measurable lead-time is ~1 month. Genuine 2-week lead-times would not be detectable. For drugs where INVIMA's monitorizacion period is < 1 month before desabastecido, we report lead = 0 which is correct but uninformative.

3. **Canonicalization is imperfect.** Dose-token stripping conflates same-formulation different-dose variants (tamoxifen 10/20 mg). Fixing this requires post-hoc rule modification, declined per pre-registration. If repeated, the canonicalization rule should retain dose tokens but normalize whitespace and unit casing.

4. **N is small for all hypotheses.** H1 N=0, H2 N=2, H3 N=4. Statistical inference is not warranted; the descriptions are descriptive only. The pre-registration explicitly forbade p-values and confidence intervals at this scale, and that decision was respected.

5. **openFDA generic_name matching is by substring.** "Carboplatin Injection" matched on `carboplatin`. Less common INNs or unconventional naming may have been missed. The 22 oncology INN whitelist limits this risk to the project's already-curated drug list.

## What this lets us claim, what it does not

**Defensible (use in funder pitch, advisor outreach, grant prose):**
- "OncoSupply's surveillance approach combines openFDA's US-based shortage registry with INVIMA's Colombia-specific monitoring. In our 2023-2025 retrospective sample, openFDA flagged oncology shortages 80–217 days before INVIMA's official shortage declaration."
- "INVIMA's `monitorizacion` status, when present, consistently preceded eventual discontinuation in our observable subset — but our 9-snapshot window starts after most 2023-vintage shortage events were already in progress, limiting the strength of this finding."

**NOT defensible (do not say):**
- "OncoSupply predicts drug shortages." — requires the deferred Plan A backtest.
- "OncoSupply has been validated against a historical event database." — H2 used 2 drugs; this is signal-precedence on a small sample, not validation.
- "The system catches shortages months before official registries." — only the openFDA + INVIMA cross-database part of the system supports this; the news pipeline is untested.

**Suggested next steps (in priority order):**
1. **H1-extension sprint:** ingest INVIMA PDFs 2022-01 → 2023-05 (~10 additional snapshots) to capture the leading edge of the 2023 shortage wave. ~1-2 days. Re-run H1.
2. **H4 (post-hoc, pre-register first):** test monitorizacion → descontinuado lead-time as a separate hypothesis. Different pre-registration document, different population filter. Avoids the H1 forbidden-move trap.
3. **Plan A unblocking:** when Marin replies (~May 16), pursue the predictive amparo backtest. If no reply by May 20, fix the ANMAT scraper bug and ingest historical bulletins.
