# Drug Shortage Risk Brief: Trastuzumab in Venezuela
## Baseline Scenario

---

## Drug Profile

**Generic/Trade Name:** Trastuzumab (Herceptin®, biosimilars)

**Therapeutic Class:** HER2-directed monoclonal antibody

**Primary Indication:** HER2-positive breast cancer, gastric cancer

**Formulation:** IV infusion concentrate (440 mg vial standard)

**Baseline Demand Estimate (Venezuela):**
- Estimated 800–1,200 HER2+ breast cancer patients annually requiring trastuzumab
- Annual consumption: ~4,000–6,000 vials (assuming standard dosing schedules)
- Market value at international pricing: ~$2.4–3.6 million USD annually

**Patent/Generic Status:** Off-patent in most jurisdictions; biosimilar availability varies by region

---

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

### **Critical Vulnerabilities — High Risk**

| Factor | Status | Impact |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **Import Dependency** | 100% | All supply sourced externally; no local manufacturing |
| **Foreign Exchange Scarcity** | Severe | VEB currency instability; USD allocation constraints limit pharmaceutical imports |
| **Regulatory Registration** | Partial | Limited product registrations; gaps in biosimilar approval pathways |
| **Cold Chain Infrastructure** | Moderate–High Risk | Inconsistent temperature monitoring in urban/peripheral facilities |
| **Distributor Network** | Fragmented | Limited private sector participation; state procurement inefficiencies |

### **Current Baseline Conditions (2024)**

- **Availability Status:** Sporadic; documented stockouts in public sector (IVSS, Ministerio de Salud)
- **Price Volatility:** Black market markups 300–500% over official pricing
- **Access Route:** Primarily private pharmacies serving affluent populations; public sector access <20%
- **Regulatory Environment:** INVEMA (National Institute of Pharmacology) registration backlog; expedited pathways limited
- **Geopolitical Factors:** International sanctions affecting import licensing and trade credit

### **Supply Bottlenecks**

1. **USD liquidity constraints** — State foreign exchange allocation insufficient to cover oncology drug imports
2. **Port/logistics delays** — Caracas port congestion; customs clearance averaging 30–60 days
3. **Manufacturer allocation** — Global suppliers prioritizing markets with reliable payment/volume commitments
4. **Intermediate product shortages** — If sourcing from regional distributors, upstream disruptions cascade quickly

## Scenario Impact Analysis

### **Baseline Scenario (No New Disruptions)**

**Assumptions:**
- Current macroeconomic conditions persist (hyperinflation, FX scarcity, sanctions framework)
- No major supply chain disruptions (e.g., manufacturer shutdowns, logistics blockades)
- Existing regulatory and procurement structures remain unchanged

### **Projected Shortage Severity: MODERATE-TO-HIGH**

| Metric | Baseline Projection |
|--------|---|
| **Availability Rate (Public Sector)** | 30–50% of demand met |
| **Patient Access Gap** | 500–800 patients annually unable to initiate/continue therapy |
| **Stockout Frequency** | 4–6 episodes per year (2–4 week durations) |
| **Average Lead Time** | 60–90 days from ordering to delivery |
| **Price Premium (vs. regional benchmarks)** | +250–400% |

### **Geographic/Institutional Variation**

- **Caracas (private sector):** Relatively stable; access for paying patients ~70%
- **Interior states:** Critical shortages; access <15%
- **IVSS/Public facilities:** Chronic deprioritization; rationing protocols in place

### **Patient Population Impact**

- **New diagnoses:** ~40% delayed treatment initiation (median 4–8 weeks)
- **Continuing patients:** ~25% experience treatment interruptions ≥2 weeks
- **Treatment abandonment:** Estimated 10–15% discontinue therapy due to access/cost barriers

### **Institutional Response**

- Health facilities increasingly shifting to off-label alternatives (e.g., hormone therapy for HER2+ ER+ cases)
- Informal private-sector procurement circumventing official channels
- Cross-border acquisition (Colombia border communities) for Venezuelan nationals

## Policy Recommendations

### **Immediate Actions (0–6 months)**

1. **Establish Strategic Pharmaceutical Reserve**
   - Dedicate quarterly FX allocation (~$500K–$750K) for trastuzumab stockpiling
   - Partner with PAHO/regional procurement mechanisms to secure bulk pricing

2. **Streamline Regulatory Approval**
   - Fast-track INVEMA registration for WHO-prequalified biosimilars
   - Reciprocal recognition agreements with EMA/Health Canada to reduce review timelines

3. **Enhance Distributor Transparency**
   - Require monthly inventory/sales reporting from private wholesalers
   - Implement price regulation caps on mark-ups (e.g., maximum +50% margin)

### **Medium-Term Reforms (6–18 months)**

4. **Regional Sourcing Diversification**
   - Negotiate direct procurement agreements with manufacturers in Argentina, Brazil (generic/biosimilar availability)
   - Join regional drug purchasing pools (e.g., UNASUR procurement initiatives if reinstated)

5. **Cold Chain & Logistics Resilience**
   - Audit and upgrade temperature-monitoring infrastructure at 8–10 central distribution hubs
   - Establish emergency logistics partnerships for rapid delivery (e.g., air transport protocols)

6. **Physician & Patient Education**
   - Clinical guidelines on treatment sequencing during supply interruptions (avoid arbitrary switches)
   - Patient registry to track treatment continuity and outcomes

### **Long-Term Strategic Measures (18+ months)**

7. **Capacity Building for Regional Production**
   - Feasibility study for fill-finish biosimilar manufacturing partnerships (e.g., technology transfer from Brazilian manufacturers)
   - Training of local oncology pharmacy workforce

8. **Macroeconomic Stabilization**
   - Advocacy for sustainable FX allocation mechanisms independent of annual budget cycles
   - Consider humanitarian import exemptions (sanctions relief) for oncology therapeutics
