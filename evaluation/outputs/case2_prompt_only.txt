# Drug Shortage Risk Brief: Trastuzumab in Venezuela
## Baseline Scenario

---

## Drug Profile

**Generic Name:** Trastuzumab (Herceptin®, biosimilars)

**Therapeutic Class:** HER2-directed monoclonal antibody

**Indication:** HER2-positive breast cancer, gastric cancer

**Formulation:** IV concentrate (440 mg vial, requires reconstitution)

**Manufacturer Footprint in Region:**
- Roche (originator, EU/US production)
- Biosimilar producers: Pfizer, Sandoz, Celltrion, Amgen (primarily Asia-Pacific/EU manufacturing)

**Critical Supply Characteristics:**
- Long manufacturing lead times (6-9 months)
- Cold-chain dependent (2-8°C)
- High price point (~$2,000–$4,000 USD per treatment cycle)
- Limited interchangeability due to biosimilar immunogenicity variability in resource-limited settings

---

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

### Current State Assessment (Baseline)

**Import Dependency:** >95%
- Venezuela has zero domestic manufacturing capacity for monoclonal antibodies
- All trastuzumab sourced through private importers, NGO channels, or residual government procurement (severely constrained since 2018)

**Key Vulnerability Factors:**

| Factor | Severity | Rationale |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **Currency Controls** | CRITICAL | Official/parallel exchange rates create arbitrage; importers lack hard currency allocation |
| **Port Infrastructure Deterioration** | CRITICAL | La Guaira and Puerto Cabello customs delays (30–90 days observed) increase product expiration risk |
| **Cold-Chain Gaps** | HIGH | Inadequate electricity; medical refrigeration failures documented in 60% of public hospitals |
| **Regulatory Authority Dysfunction** | HIGH | INVIMA-equivalent (Ministry of Health) lacks import inspection capacity |
| **Private Sector Fragmentation** | MEDIUM-HIGH | <5 licensed importers; supply concentrated among 2–3 entities with inconsistent stock |
| **Payment System Instability** | CRITICAL | Sanctions restrict wire transfers; SWIFT access unreliable for pharmaceutical importers |

**Baseline Availability Metrics:**
- Stock-outs in public sector: **chronic (>75% of months in 2023)**
- Private sector availability: **episodic (50–70% of quarters)**
- Price volatility (parallel market): **±200–400% YoY**

---

## Scenario Impact Analysis

### Baseline Scenario Definition
No major new disruptions; continuation of current chronic undersupply and fragmented market conditions through 12-month forecast horizon.

### Projected Impact (12-Month Outlook)

**Public Sector Impact:**
- **Stock-out Duration:** 60–80% of months
- **Patient Population Affected:** ~2,500–3,500 women initiating/continuing HER2+ breast cancer treatment annually
- **Access Gap:** 70–85% of diagnosed patients unable to access trastuzumab through public oncology networks
- **Treatment Consequence:** Significant disease progression and increased mortality in untreated/under-treated cohorts

**Private Sector Impact:**
- **Availability:** Maintained for high-income patients via private importers; intermittent supply
- **Price Range:** $2,500–$6,000+ USD per cycle (3–4× global reference prices)
- **Out-of-Pocket Burden:** Unsustainable for middle-income Venezuelans; affordability barrier affects 90%+ of non-elite population

**Supply Continuity Risk:**
- **Mild deterioration expected:** 5–10% further constraint attributed to:
  - Ongoing dollar scarcity for importers
  - Incremental port congestion
  - Marginal currency devaluation (8–12% additional depreciation vs. USD projected)

**No acute shortage spike anticipated** absent external shocks (new sanctions, major importer insolvency, regional logistics collapse).

## Policy Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (0–3 months)

1. **Establish Emergency Procurement Mechanism**
   - Activate direct government-to-manufacturer agreements (e.g., PAHO revolving fund) to bypass private importer bottleneck
   - Target: secured quarterly allocations of 200–300 treatment units for public sector
   - Funding requirement: ~$500,000–$750,000 USD per quarter (requires international financing)

2. **Cold-Chain Audit & Rehabilitation**
   - Conduct rapid assessment of oncology center refrigeration infrastructure
   - Deploy solar-powered backup units to top 15 treatment sites
   - Cost: ~$100,000 USD; timeline: 8–12 weeks

3. **Port Expediting Protocol**
   - Establish pharmaceutical-priority lane at customs (Caracas, Puerto Cabello)
   - Target: <14-day clearance for oncology drugs
   - Regulatory burden: low; requires inter-agency MOU

### Medium-Term Measures (3–12 months)

4. **Biosimilar Equivalence Recognition**
   - Formally adopt EMA/WHO prequalified biosimilar list (Pfizer, Sandoz, Celltrion products)
   - Negotiate tiered pricing with regional wholesalers (EMPSAA, established vendors in Colombia/Brazil)
   - Expected savings: 20–35% vs. originator; improves supply resilience via multiple sources

5. **PAHO Bulk Procurement**
   - Include Venezuela in regional pooled procurement for HER2-targeted therapies
   - Leverage volume discounts across Central America/Caribbean
   - Estimated price reduction: 15–25%; higher order predictability

6. **Importer Capacity Building**
   - Provide credit lines/guarantees to 2–3 licensed importers for strategic reserves
   - Training: regulatory compliance, cold-chain management
   - Mechanism: IDB or CAF concessional finance (~$200,000 USD)

### Longer-Term Structural Reforms (6–18 months)

7. **Currency Stabilization & Importer Access**
   - Advocate for pharmaceutical sector inclusion in priority foreign exchange allocation
   - Establish escrow accounts for verified oncology purchases
   - Requires central bank cooperation; political feasibility: contested
