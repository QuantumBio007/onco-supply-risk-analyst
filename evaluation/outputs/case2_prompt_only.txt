# Drug Shortage Risk Brief: Trastuzumab in Venezuela
## Baseline Scenario

---

## Drug Profile

**Drug Name:** Trastuzumab (Herceptin®, biosimilars)

**Therapeutic Indication:** HER2-positive breast cancer, gastric cancer

**Global Supply Context:**
- Manufactured primarily in Europe (Switzerland, Germany) and US
- Multiple biosimilar manufacturers now active (Roche, Sandoz, Amgen, Pfizer, others)
- WHO prequalified biosimilars available
- Typical shelf-life: 2–3 years (refrigerated)

**Venezuelan Market Characteristics:**
- Estimated annual demand: 200–400 vials (12 mg/vial standard dosing)
- HER2+ breast cancer incidence: ~800–1,200 cases/year
- Treatment coverage historically <20% due to cost and access barriers
- Price range (retail): $800–2,500 USD per vial (when available)

---

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

### Baseline Risk Level: **HIGH**

| Vulnerability Factor | Status | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Import Dependency** | 100% imported | No local manufacturing capacity |
| **Currency/Payment Systems** | Severe bolivar depreciation; forex controls active | Importers lack hard currency allocation |
| **Port Infrastructure** | Limited cold-chain logistics; Customs delays 4–8 weeks | Temperature-excursion risk during import |
| **Regulatory Pathway** | INVIMA registration gaps; slow approval process | Biosimilars not consistently registered |
| **Distributor Network** | Consolidated (2–3 major private importers) | Bottleneck if distributor exits market |
| **Price Controls** | Government price ceiling (non-enforced due to shortage) | No profitable pricing model; disincentivizes importation |

### Root Causes of Baseline Shortage:
1. **Macroeconomic collapse:** Foreign exchange scarcity prevents importers from purchasing international stock
2. **Profit Margin Erosion:** Official price caps vs. cost-of-goods + logistics makes import economically unviable
3. **Market Informality:** Drugs obtained through gray-market channels or cross-border smuggling (Colombia, Curaçao)
4. **Insurance Non-reimbursement:** Public/private insurance does not cover trastuzumab; out-of-pocket only
5. **Geopolitical Sanctions:** US/EU sanctions limit some financial transaction pathways

## Scenario Impact Analysis

### Current State (Baseline)

**Availability Status:**
- **Public Sector (MPPS hospitals):** Negligible stock; sporatic donations only
- **Private Sector:** Intermittent availability; estimated 60–80% of demand unmet
- **Estimated Shortage:** ~70–85% of clinical need

**Patient Impact:**
- Eligible patients: ~300–400/year
- Patients accessing therapy: ~60–100/year
- Off-label delay-of-treatment: 6–18 months common; disease progression during wait

**Economic Impact:**
- Import value foregone: $240,000–1,000,000 USD/year
- Black-market markup: 200–400% above international price
- Forced treatment abandonment → preventable mortality increase

**Healthcare System Burden:**
- Oncology centers unable to offer standard-of-care HER2-directed therapy
- Reputational impact on public health messaging
- Increased late-stage diagnoses requiring costlier palliative care

## Policy Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (0–6 months)

1. **Expedited Biosimilar Registration**
   - Fast-track INVIMA approval for WHO-prequalified trastuzumab biosimilars (Sandoz, Amgen)
   - Reduce documentation burden to 120 days
   - *Rationale:* Expand supplier base; lower-cost alternatives

2. **Humanitarian Import Exemption**
   - Establish tariff/VAT waiver for trastuzumab imports destined to public oncology centers
   - Create simplified customs pathway (pre-clearance documentation)
   - *Rationale:* Reduce cost barrier; accelerate delivery

3. **Donor Coordination Mechanism**
   - Formalize partnership with international pharma companies (Roche, Sandoz) for direct donation programs
   - Engage PAHO/WHO for technical assistance in inventory management
   - *Rationale:* Supplement commercial supply without foreign-exchange outlay

### Medium-Term (6–18 months)

4. **Tiered Pricing & Subsidy Model**
   - Negotiate volume discounts with registered biosimilar suppliers
   - Establish government subsidy pool (even limited budget) to reduce out-of-pocket cost barrier
   - *Rationale:* Increase demand visibility; improve importer planning

5. **Regional Supply Coordination**
   - Partner with Colombian/Ecuadorian regulators to establish shared biosimilar procurement (economies of scale)
   - Explore ALBA/regional health agreements for preferential access
   - *Rationale:* Leverage larger regional market for better pricing

6. **Cold-Chain Infrastructure Audit**
   - Assess port storage, transport, and hospital pharmacy conditions
   - Identify bottleneck upgrades (refrigeration units, monitoring systems)
   - *Rationale:* Reduce wastage from temperature excursions

### Long-Term (18+ months)

7. **Insurance & Reimbursement Reform**
   - Include trastuzumab in public health oncology treatment guidelines (NORMAS, CNF)
   - Pressure private insurers to cover HER2-directed therapy as standard-of-care
   - *Rationale:* Stabilize demand signal; create predictable market

8. **Oncology Drug Procurement Authority**
   - Create centralized government procurement office (or NGO-led consortium) to negotiate bulk trastuzumab supply
   - Establish 6-month forward-purchase commitment to ensure stable inventory
   - *Rationale:* Reduce single-distributor dependency; improve negotiating power
