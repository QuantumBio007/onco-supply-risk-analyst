# Drug Shortage Risk Brief: Carboplatin in Venezuela
**Combined Shock Scenario**

---

## Drug Profile

**Drug Name:** Carboplatin (CBDCA)
**Therapeutic Class:** Platinum-based chemotherapy agent
**WHO Essential Medicines List:** Yes (cancer therapy)
**Primary Oncology Indications:** Ovarian cancer, lung cancer, head/neck cancer, bladder cancer

**Venezuelan Market Context:**
- Carboplatin is critical for advanced-stage malignancies in Venezuela where late-stage diagnoses predominate
- No domestic manufacturing capacity; 100% import-dependent
- Typical monthly consumption estimate: 150–250 vials across tertiary care centers (pre-2018 baseline)
- Current consumption likely <50% of pre-crisis levels due to patient access collapse

**Key Supply Characteristics:**
- Multiple global manufacturers (Hospira/Pfizer, Teva, generic suppliers in India, China)
- Moderate price volatility; platinum commodity cost fluctuations (5–15% quarterly variation)
- Requires cold-chain logistics and specialized oncology pharmacy infrastructure
- 6–10 week lead time from order to Venezuelan port (direct routes limited)

---

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

### **Critical Vulnerability Factors**

| Vulnerability Layer | Current State | Combined Shock Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Foreign Currency Access** | Severe (CADIVI/DICOM collapse) | Acute: Unable to secure USD for international procurement; parallel market rates 40–60× official rates |
| **Port Infrastructure** | Severely degraded | Extreme: Port of La Guaira operating at <30% capacity; customs clearance backlogs 4–8 weeks |
| **Import Documentation** | Administratively chaotic | Critical: INVEX authorization increasingly arbitrary; pharmaceutical import licenses effectively frozen |
| **Cold Chain** | Fragmented/unreliable | High: Electricity blackouts interrupt refrigeration (estimated 180+ days/year in 2023); vial viability compromised |
| **Healthcare System Purchasing Power** | Collapsed | Extreme: Public hospitals unable to allocate budget; private institutions limited to patient out-of-pocket funding |
| **Regional Alternatives** | Limited | Moderate: Colombia, Brazil suppliers accessible but require hard currency; no ALBA/MERCOSUR preferential supply agreements active |

### **Choke Points Under Combined Shock**

1. **Currency Crisis Amplification:** Combined shock (simultaneous crude oil price collapse + continued economic contraction) reduces government forex reserves below $3B, triggering prioritization away from oncology drugs toward fuel/food imports.

2. **Port Congestion Cascade:** Political instability + lack of investment = port workforce attrition and equipment failures; carboplatin shipments face 60–90 day delays even if procurement authorized.

3. **Regulatory Whiplash:** Concurrent administrative transitions increase INVEX denials for "non-essential" medications; oncology drugs deprioritized relative to antibiotics/antiretrovirals.

4. **Healthcare System Financial Collapse:** Combined shock deepens institutional insolvency; public hospitals' ability to co-finance drug purchases via patient cost-sharing becomes impossible for 70%+ of patient population.

## Scenario Impact Analysis

### **Combined Shock Definition**
**Simultaneous occurrence:** 
- Oil price sustained <$45/bbl (reducing government revenue 35–40%)
- Political instability event (leadership transition uncertainty)
- Regional pandemic-related supply chain disruption (manufacturing delays, shipping route closures)
- No new IMF/international financial facility agreement

**Timeline & Severity:**

| Phase | Timeline | Carboplatin Availability | Clinical Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Immediate (0–6 weeks)** | Shock onset | 40–50% of baseline; existing hospital inventory partially depleted | Postponement of non-urgent treatments; emergency protocols for advanced ovarian cancer |
| **Acute (6–14 weeks)** | Post-shock adjustment | 10–20% of baseline; critical stockout at 3+ tertiary centers | Treatment discontinuation; disease progression in mid-treatment patients |
| **Sustained (14+ weeks)** | Chronic crisis phase | 5–10% of baseline or complete unavailability for 6+ week periods | Effective unavailability for public sector; private access limited to ultra-high-income patients |

### **Quantitative Shortfall Projection**

**Current estimated Venezuelan demand:** 
- Public sector: ~120 vials/month
- Private sector: ~80 vials/month
- **Total: ~200 vials/month**

**Under combined shock (weeks 6–14):**
- Projected supply: 20–40 vials/month
- **Shortfall: 160–180 vials/month (80–90% deficit)**

**Patient Population at Risk:**
- Estimated 180–220 patients actively requiring carboplatin therapy interrupted or delayed
- Secondary impact: ~400–500 patients with planned treatments deferred indefinitely

### **Affected Patient Demographics**

- **Ovarian cancer patients:** 60–70% (typically advanced stage at diagnosis; carboplatin backbone therapy)
- **Lung cancer patients:** 20–25% (predominantly non-small-cell, stage IIIB–IV)
- **Head/neck, bladder cancers:** 5–10%
- **Socioeconomic distribution:** 85%+ are public-sector patients with no capacity for international pharmacy access

### **Secondary Supply Chain Effects**

- Diversion of available carboplatin to private high-cost oncology centers
- Informal/unregulated distribution channels emerging (quality/safety unverified)
- Cross-border purchasing from Colombian suppliers (accessible only to ~5–10% of Venezuelan patients)
- Potential substitution with less-effective non-platinum regimens (toxicity/efficacy trade-offs)
