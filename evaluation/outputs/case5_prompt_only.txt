# Drug Shortage Risk Brief: Carboplatin in Venezuela
## Combined Shock Scenario

---

## Drug Profile

**Drug:** Carboplatin (platinum-based chemotherapy agent)

**Therapeutic Class:** Alkylating agent; first-line treatment for ovarian, lung, and testicular cancers

**Formulation in LAC:** IV solution (50 mg/5mL, 150 mg/15mL vials)

**Typical Venezuelan Demand:** Estimated 800–1,200 treatment cycles/year (based on cancer incidence ~2,500 new cases annually requiring platinum agents)

**Shelf Life:** 24 months (unopened); 8 hours post-reconstitution

**Price Sensitivity:** High—generic carboplatin costs USD 50–150/vial; branded formulations USD 200–400/vial

---

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

### Current Baseline Fragility (Pre-Shock)
- **Manufacturing:** Venezuela has zero domestic production capacity; 100% import-dependent
- **Primary Sources:** Argentina, Mexico (regional); India, China (global manufacturers)
- **Import Mechanisms:** 
  - Government-regulated acquisitions through FARMACEI (state agency) → severely constrained by USD shortage
  - Private sector imports → restricted by currency controls (SIMADI rates >2,000 VEF/USD as of 2024)
  - Humanitarian channels → limited and inconsistent

### **Combined Shock Scenario Stressors**

| Shock Component | Impact Mechanism |
|---|---|
| **USD liquidity crisis** | Government allocation to carboplatin frozen; foreign currency reserves depleted for fuel/food |
| **Regional supply disruption** | Argentine/Mexican suppliers reduce credit terms or halt exports due to payment defaults |
| **Port/logistics collapse** | Puerto de La Guaira throughput ↓80%; shipping delays 4–8 weeks; demurrage costs unaffordable |
| **Cold chain breakdown** | Refrigerated transport failures; warehouse temperature excursions; vial wastage 15–25% |
| **Healthcare system contraction** | Hospital procurement budgets collapse; oncology centers consolidate; demand volatility |

### Vulnerability Score: **CRITICAL (9.2/10)**

---

## Scenario Impact Analysis

### Timeline & Progression

**Phase 1: Weeks 1–4 (Immediate)**
- Current inventory (estimated 6–8 weeks at major hospitals) sufficient
- Government payment orders to suppliers frozen; no new shipments authorized
- Private importers attempt emergency purchases; fail to secure USD at official rates
- **Patient Impact:** None yet; elective treatments postponed preemptively

**Phase 2: Weeks 5–12 (Acute Shortage)**
- Hospital stocks fall below 2 weeks; FARMACEI announces "temporary rationing"
- Regional manufacturers (Argentina) halt shipments due to USD non-payment (~$2–4M owed)
- Illicit market emerges; counterfeit/substandard carboplatin detected in Caracas, Maracaibo
- **Patient Impact:** 
  - 40–60% of eligible patients denied treatment initiation
  - Ongoing patients receive dose reductions or extended intervals (therapeutic risk)
  - Average delay to treatment: 6–8 weeks

**Phase 3: Weeks 13+ (Chronic Crisis)**
- Only humanitarian imports via PAHO/ICRC reach Venezuela (~200 cycles/month)
- Estimated supply covers 20–25% of demand
- Healthcare workers report patient deaths from treatment interruption (ovarian cancer progression, etc.)
- **Patient Impact:**
  - Cumulative treatment interruptions: 60–80% of cohort
  - Estimated preventable deaths/year: 150–250 (assuming 40% progression-related mortality if untreated >8 weeks)

## Policy Recommendations

### **Immediate (0–30 days)**

1. **Currency Allocation Emergency**
   - Designate carboplatin + other essential oncology drugs as "vital medicines" in CADIVI/SITME currency auctions
   - Allocate minimum USD 5–8M/month for chemotherapy procurement
   - *Responsible party:* Ministry of Health + Central Bank of Venezuela

2. **Humanitarian Channel Activation**
   - Formalize agreements with PAHO, ICRC, and Global Fund for direct drug shipments to government oncology centers
   - Streamline customs procedures (zero-tariff import status)
   - *Responsible party:* Ministry of Health + INTERPOL (drug authentication)

3. **Inventory Visibility**
   - Mandate real-time reporting of carboplatin stock levels across all oncology units
   - Deploy emergency "drug pooling" system across hospitals to optimize distribution
   - *Responsible party:* FARMACEI + Hospital Network Coordination Office

### **Short-term (1–3 months)**

4. **Regional Credit Agreements**
   - Negotiate extended payment terms (90–120 days) with Argentine/Mexican manufacturers
   - Offer commodity exports (oil derivatives, minerals) as barter if USD unavailable
   - *Responsible party:* Ministry of Health + Ministry of Commerce

5. **Counterfeit Surveillance**
   - Establish rapid drug authentication lab (HPLC/mass spectrometry) at main distribution hub
   - Train pharmacy staff in visual inspection protocols
   - Deploy patient hotline for adverse event reporting linked to suspected falsified drugs
   - *Responsible party:* INVEMA (regulatory authority) + Medical Colleges

6. **Clinical Protocol Adaptation**
   - Issue temporary guidelines permitting:
     - Dose optimization studies (lower-frequency, higher-dose schedules if supply-constrained)
     - Substitution pathways to alternative platinum agents (cisplatin, oxaliplatin) where oncologically appropriate
   - *Responsible party:* Ministry of Health + National Oncology Society (caveat: clinical substitution decisions remain with treating physicians)

### **Medium-term (3–12 months)**

7. **Supply Chain Diversification**
   - Establish framework agreements with 3+ global suppliers (India, China, EU) to reduce single-source risk
   - Create strategic carboplatin reserve (60-day supply) at FARMACEI central warehouse
   - *Responsible party:* FARMACEI + Ministry of Health
