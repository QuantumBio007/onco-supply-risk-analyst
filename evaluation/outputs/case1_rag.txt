## Drug Profile

Cisplatin is a platinum-based chemotherapy agent on the WHO Model List of Essential Medicines. It is generic and off-patent. Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient (API) supply is concentrated in two countries: India and China. There is no domestic API manufacturing in Argentina. Generic cisplatin products are subject to ANMAT (Administración Nacional de Medicamentos, Alimentos y Tecnología Médica) registration requirements. Historical context: cisplatin experienced periodic global supply disruptions, including shortages reported in the United States in 2023 linked to manufacturing consolidation at Indian API suppliers.

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

**Import Dependency & API Concentration**
Argentina has no domestic cisplatin API or formulation manufacturing and relies entirely on imports. Global API supply is concentrated in India and China — any export restriction, regulatory action, or geopolitical disruption simultaneously affects all generic manufacturers globally.

**Currency Risk**
Oncology drugs are traded in USD globally, while Argentine procurement budgets (public hospitals, obras sociales, and PAMI) are denominated in Argentine pesos. Chronic currency devaluation reduces purchasing power across the entire system. Suppliers facing delayed payments reduce credit extension to Argentine buyers, compressing distributor inventory levels.

**Regulatory & Registration Barriers**
ANMAT maintains a registration requirement for all commercially distributed drugs. If the only registered manufacturer of a specific cisplatin formulation exits the market, the standard supply pathway is interrupted until another manufacturer completes the registration process — a process that can take months to years.

**Procurement Fragmentation & Visibility Gap**
Argentina's multi-channel procurement landscape (public hospitals, obras sociales, PAMI, private) creates structural visibility gaps. No single entity has complete, integrated, real-time operational visibility into national cisplatin stock across all channels. Traceability systems exist but do not provide actionable inventory visibility in operational time.

**Generic Market Consolidation**
Generic market price pressure reduces manufacturer margins, leading some producers to exit the market. Single-source or dual-source API scenarios for specific formulations are common, increasing concentration risk.

**Interpretation**
Under baseline conditions, cisplatin supply in Argentina is projected to remain within acceptable operational range, with 7.0 stockout days per year and a unit service level exceeding 98%. However, the 74% probability of experiencing at least one stockout event during the year indicates that even under normal conditions, intermittent supply disruptions are a structural feature of the system, not exceptional occurrences. These brief stockouts are absorbed by emergency mechanisms (inter-service drug loans, provincial drug banks, temporary protocol modification), but this reliance on informal logistics creates vulnerability to scaling: a longer disruption would exceed these mechanisms' capacity.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Establish National Strategic Cisplatin Reserve**: Create a 60–90 day buffer stock held at the national level (e.g., under PAMI or a dedicated central pharmacy) to absorb lead-time variability and API supply disruptions without requiring emergency administrative action.

2. **Implement Integrated Inventory Visibility System**: Develop a real-time, interoperable national oncology drug inventory dashboard spanning public hospitals, obras sociales, and PAMI. Distinguish between traceability (regulatory unit tracking) and actionable visibility (stock availability by location and transferability). Empower a designated coordinator (e.g., ANMAT or Ministry of Health) to direct inter-channel inventory reallocation during shortages.

3. **Coordinate Procurement Across Multi-Channel System**: Establish a procurement coordination mechanism that links public hospitals, obras sociales, and PAMI to consolidate demand, reduce fragmentation, and improve negotiating leverage with suppliers. Synchronize tender cycles to avoid staggered demand that fragments supplier allocation.

4. **Currency Risk Hedging & Budget Protection**: Protect oncology procurement budgets from peso devaluation through USD-denominated budget reserves or exchange rate hedging arrangements. Insulate hospital and payer purchasing power from short-term currency volatility.

5. **Support ANMAT Registration Pathways**: Maintain expedited registration tracks for alternative cisplatin manufacturers and formulations. Pre-approve backup suppliers to reduce the time lag if the primary manufacturer exits the market.

6. **Monitor API Export Controls**: Establish an early-warning mechanism for API export restrictions or regulatory actions in India and China. Coordinate with WHO and regional agencies to anticipate global supply shocks.
