# Drug Shortage Risk Brief: Doxorubicin in Colombia
## Currency Devaluation Scenario

---

## Drug Profile

**Drug:** Doxorubicin (doxorubicin hydrochloride)
**Therapeutic Class:** Anthracycline chemotherapy agent
**Formulations in Colombian Market:** 
- IV solution 2 mg/mL (10 mL, 25 mL, 50 mL vials)
- Liposomal formulation (limited availability)

**Colombian Usage Context:**
- Essential medicine for breast cancer, lymphomas, and pediatric malignancies
- Estimated annual demand: ~80–120 kg active pharmaceutical ingredient (API)
- Primary suppliers: European manufacturers (Pfizer, Teva), Indian generic producers
- Typical import value: USD $1.2–1.8 million annually

**Regulatory Status:** Colombian INVIMA-approved; included in national cancer formularies

---

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

### Import Dependency Structure
- **80%+ of doxorubicin** consumed in Colombia is imported (finished product or API)
- Primary source countries: Spain, Italy, Germany (finished vials); India, China (API/intermediates)
- Average lead time: 45–90 days from international supplier to patient access
- Colombian currency (COP) is moderately volatile against USD; historical 12-month depreciation cycles range 8–15%

### Currency Devaluation Impact Mechanisms

| Vulnerability Factor | Mechanism | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| **Import Cost Inflation** | 1 USD = ~4,200 COP (baseline) → depreciation to ~5,000+ COP increases landed costs 15–20% | HIGH |
| **Budget Absorption Capacity** | Hospital budgets fixed in COP; procurement officers face mid-cycle shortfalls | HIGH |
| **Distributor Liquidity** | Wholesalers operate on 30–45 day payment terms; currency fluctuation squeezes working capital | MEDIUM-HIGH |
| **Price Freeze Regulations** | Colombian price controls limit manufacturer/distributor ability to pass costs to public sector | HIGH |
| **Forward Purchasing Barriers** | Hospitals lack foreign exchange reserves for advance procurement | MEDIUM |

### Critical Pinch Points

1. **Public Sector Procurement (50% of market)**
   - Ministry of Health (MinSalud) and regional health secretariats control ~60% of institutional demand
   - Budget reallocation delays typical; legislative approval required for emergency funding
   - Purchasing power contracts negotiated annually in COP; depreciation erodes negotiating position

2. **Private Oncology Centers (40% of market)**
   - Greater flexibility but dependent on patient out-of-pocket payment + insurance reimbursement
   - Insurance companies (EPS) may deny claims if doxorubicin unavailable, forcing hospital stock depletion
   - Reduced patient purchasing capacity during macroeconomic stress

3. **Distribution Network (10% risk)**
   - 3 major pharmaceutical wholesalers (Cofarfarma, Droguería Colsubsidio, Cardinal) concentrate 70% distribution
   - Single-source dependency on key intermediary suppliers increases fragility

---

## Scenario Impact Analysis

### Assumed Devaluation Parameters
- **Trigger:** COP depreciates 18–22% against USD over 6–12 months (moderate macroeconomic shock, comparable to 2014–2015 commodity collapse)
- **Timeline:** Immediate effects (0–3 months); cascading effects (3–9 months)

### Phase 1: Immediate (Weeks 1–8)
- **Supplier Response:** International manufacturers increase prices 12–18% for new shipments
- **Institutional Action:** Public sector procurement freezes pending budget reallocation (typical delay: 4–8 weeks)
- **Stock Status:** Hospital inventory at 60–75% of normal levels; private centers begin prioritization protocols
- **Risk Level:** MODERATE

### Phase 2: Acute (Months 2–4)
- **Public Sector Shortfall:** MinSalud allocates emergency funds, but approval timelines compress procurement cycle
- **Private Sector Strain:** Insurance denials increase 20–30%; patient out-of-pocket costs rise; demand shifts to public hospitals
- **Distributor Contraction:** Wholesalers reduce open orders; smaller regional hospitals deprioritized
- **Clinical Impact Onset:** Non-urgent cancer cases experience 2–4 week treatment delays; oncology units implement rationing protocols
- **Risk Level:** HIGH

### Phase 3: Structural Stress (Months 4–12)
- **Sustained Shortage Probability:** 35–50% if devaluation persists without currency stabilization
- **Alternative Procurement:** Hospitals attempt cross-border acquisition (Venezuela, Ecuador, Peru); informal importation increases; regulatory compliance deteriorates
- **Patient Outcomes:** Treatment delays linked to stage progression, reduced survival rates in early-intervention cancers
- **Workforce Impact:** Oncology nurses reassigned; clinical trials paused
- **Risk Level:** CRITICAL
