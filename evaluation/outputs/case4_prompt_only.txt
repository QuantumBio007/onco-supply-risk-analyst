# Drug Shortage Risk Brief: Doxorubicin in Colombia
## Currency Devaluation Scenario

---

## Drug Profile

**Drug:** Doxorubicin (doxorubicina)
**Therapeutic Class:** Anthracycline chemotherapy agent
**Primary Oncology Indications:** Breast cancer, lymphomas, sarcomas, multiple myeloma
**Formulations in Colombian Market:** 
- Injectable solution (10 mg/10 mL, 50 mg/25 mL vials)
- Liposomal formulation (limited availability)

**Current Colombian Market Position:**
- Essential medicine on INVIMA approved list
- Primarily supplied via multinational manufacturers (Pfizer, Actavis, generic manufacturers)
- Estimated monthly consumption: 800–1,200 vials across public/private sector
- Average retail price: COP 85,000–120,000 per standard vial (pre-devaluation baseline)

---

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

### Import Dependency Structure
- **80–85%** of doxorubicin supply enters Colombia as finished pharmaceutical product
- Primary sourcing: Mexico, USA, India, and European manufacturers
- Secondary sourcing: Brazil, Panama distribution hubs
- Domestic manufacturing: Minimal (formulation only; active pharmaceutical ingredient [API] imported)

### Currency Devaluation Exposure

**Mechanism of Vulnerability:**
- Colombian peso (COP) historically volatile; 2022–2023 experienced 15–22% depreciation against USD
- Doxorubicin priced internationally in USD; devaluation increases landed cost by proportional percentage
- **Cost multiplier:** 20% COP depreciation = ~20% cost increase in COP terms

**Affected Supply Chain Nodes:**

| Node | Impact Severity | Rationale |
|------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Import Licensing & Payment** | High | Manufacturers require USD payment; COP weakening delays orders |
| **Wholesaler Inventory** | Medium | Negative margins force inventory drawdown; restocking delays |
| **Hospital/Clinical Purchasing** | High | Budget caps (especially public sector) trigger purchasing freezes |
| **Price Regulation** | Medium | INVIMA/Min. Salud price controls lag devaluation; margin compression |

### Key Vulnerability Factors

1. **Public Sector Budget Constraints**
   - Colombian health system (EPS/IPS) operates on fixed annual budgets
   - Currency devaluation consumes oncology drug budgets without volume increase
   - Estimated lag time for budget adjustment: 3–6 months

2. **Manufacturer Pricing Dynamics**
   - Multinational suppliers often maintain USD-denominated pricing
   - Pass-through to COP prices occurs within 30–60 days
   - Smaller importers may absorb losses, reducing inventory allocation

3. **Parallel Market Risk**
   - Price spikes incentivize diversion to private sector/neighboring countries (Venezuela, Ecuador)
   - INVIMA enforcement capacity limited; illicit export estimated at 5–8% of supply

4. **API Supply Shock Lag**
   - Raw material doxorubicin (from India/China) subject to own currency fluctuations
   - Manufacturer production lead times: 90–120 days
   - Devaluation signals may trigger pre-emptive supplier price increases

## Scenario Impact Analysis

### Assumption: 15–20% COP Depreciation Over 6 Months

**Timeline & Cascade Effects:**

**Months 0–1 (Announcement/Initial Shock)**
- Manufacturers signal price increases (10–15% in COP terms)
- Wholesalers accelerate orders in anticipation (temporary spike in demand)
- Public sector procurement officers implement informal purchasing freezes pending budget guidance

**Months 1–3 (Price Transmission)**
- Retail price increases 15–18% in COP
- Hospital acquisition costs rise; smaller public facilities defer non-urgent oncology cases
- Private-sector demand shifts toward generic formulations (cost differential)
- INVIMA receives ~50–80 formal price increase requests

**Months 3–6 (Supply Stress)**

**Shortage Probability: MEDIUM (50–65%)**

**Specific Risk Scenarios:**

1. **Moderate Shortage (Probability: 40%)**
   - Public sector supply gaps of 20–30% in second/third line oncology centers
   - Rationing protocols implemented in ~30% of public hospitals
   - Average delay in patient access: 2–4 weeks
   - **Impact:** Non-critical cases deferred; adherence interruptions in 15–25% of ongoing treatments

2. **Severe Shortage (Probability: 15–20%)**
   - Triggered if devaluation exceeds 25% OR simultaneous production disruption at major supplier
   - Supply gaps reach 40–50% in public sector
   - Regional variation: Caribbean coast & rural departments more severely affected
   - **Impact:** Treatment abandonment in 8–12% of cases; switch to alternative chemotherapy

3. **No Shortage (Probability: 25–30%)**
   - Government emergency price controls + budget supplementation limit impact
   - Private sector absorbs demand shift
   - Wholesaler emergency reserves deployed

### Geographic & Facility Vulnerability Map

**High Risk Zones:**
- Medellín (Antioquia): Large oncology patient population; high public-sector dependency
- Bogotá: Budget constraints in smaller public IPS networks
- Caribbean departments (Bolívar, Atlántico): Limited import infrastructure; 1–2 month supply buffer

**Lower Risk Zones:**
- Private medical centers in Bogotá/Cali: Absorb cost increases
- Large referral hospitals: Pre-positioned strategic reserves

### Population Impact Estimate

- **Estimated oncology patients on doxorubicin-containing regimens:** 8,000–12,000 (public sector majority)
- **Patients potentially affected by 3+ month shortage:** 2,000–3,500 (25–30% of public cohort)
- **Cases with treatment discontinuation >30 days:** 300–600 (3–5% of total population)
