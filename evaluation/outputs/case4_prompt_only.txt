# Drug Shortage Risk Brief: Doxorubicin in Colombia
## Currency Devaluation Scenario

---

## Drug Profile

**Drug Name:** Doxorubicin (doxorubicin hydrochloride)  
**Therapeutic Class:** Anthracycline chemotherapy agent  
**Primary Indications:** Breast cancer, lymphomas, sarcomas, multiple myeloma  
**Formulations in Colombian Market:** 
- IV solution (10 mg/mL, 50 mg vials)
- Liposomal formulation (limited availability)

**Colombia-Specific Usage:**
- Annual estimated demand: ~2,500–3,200 units (vials)
- Primary patient population: Public sector (SGSSS) and private oncology centers
- Critical status: No therapeutic substitutes for many indications

---

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

### Current Structure
| Element | Status |
|---------|--------|
| **Primary Sources** | Multinational manufacturers (India, China, EU); minimal domestic production |
| **Import Dependency** | ~95% (primarily through regional distributors) |
| **Key Suppliers** | Pfizer, Hospira/Pfizer, Fresenius Kabi, Actavis; Indian API suppliers |
| **Lead Time** | 60–90 days typical; 120+ days during constraints |
| **Storage Capacity** | Limited cold-chain infrastructure outside major centers |

### Currency Devaluation Impact Mechanisms

**1. Import Cost Escalation**
- COP/USD weakness directly increases acquisition costs for importers
- 15–20% peso devaluation = ~18–25% cost increase for USD-denominated contracts
- Compressed importer margins reduce inventory buffer stock

**2. Manufacturer Pricing Rebalancing**
- Multinational suppliers repricing contracts to offset exchange losses
- Possible temporary fulfillment delays while price renegotiations occur
- Priority allocation to higher-margin markets (USA, EU) likely

**3. Liquidity Constraints**
- Distributors' purchasing power diminished; reduced forward-buying capacity
- Payment term compression (Net 30/60 → Net 15/30) reduces working capital for local suppliers
- Public sector payment delays (90–180 days typical) compound cash flow stress

**4. Regulatory/Institutional Factors**
- INVIMA pricing approvals lag behind exchange rate changes (~3–6 month lag)
- SGSSS budget caps may not accommodate price increases; reimbursement restrictions
- Public procurement contract prices become non-competitive; suppliers withdraw or reduce allocations

## Scenario Impact Analysis

### Baseline Risk (Without Currency Shock)
- **Shortage Probability:** Low–Moderate (15–20%)
- **Duration Risk:** 2–4 weeks (isolated disruptions)

### Under 15–20% COP Devaluation Scenario

| Timeline | Likelihood | Impact Severity | Key Indicators |
|----------|------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Weeks 1–2** | High (80%) | Mild–Moderate | Price increases; supply alerts issued |
| **Weeks 3–8** | Moderate (50%) | Moderate–High | Stockouts in public sector; private sector rationing |
| **Weeks 9–16** | Moderate (40%) | High | Widespread shortage; treatment delays; patient impact |
| **Months 4–6+** | Low–Moderate (20%) | Severe (if sustained) | Chronic shortage; therapeutic cascading |

### Affected Patient Populations
- **Most Vulnerable:** SGSSS enrollees (public sector); low-income beneficiaries in peripheral departments
- **Duration of Delayed Treatment:** Estimated 4–12 weeks for affected patients
- **Estimated Patient Exposure:** 800–1,200 patients per shortage month

### Critical Pressure Points
1. **Pediatric oncology units** — lower volumes; narrower procurement flexibility
2. **Regional hospitals** (outside Bogotá, Medellín, Cali) — limited emergency access to parallel suppliers
3. **Combination therapy regimens** (e.g., AC → AC-taxane) — inability to initiate cohort treatments

## Policy Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (Weeks 1–4)
1. **INVIMA/Ministry of Health Coordination**
   - Activate supply alert protocol; trigger early warning communications to SGSSS administrators
   - Expedite pricing authority review (pre-approval of 10–15% cost adjustments to prevent inventory freeze)
   - Establish task force: MINSALUD, INVIMA, ADRES, major oncology networks

2. **Procurement Optimization**
   - Public sector: shift to 90-day forward purchasing (if budget allows) before devaluation deepens
   - Regional pooled procurement: aggregate demand across 2–3 departments to negotiate volume discounts in local currency

3. **Inventory Transparency**
   - Mandate weekly supply reporting from distributors and hospital pharmacies
   - Map regional stockpiles; facilitate inter-hospital reallocation mechanisms

### Medium-term Interventions (Weeks 4–12)
4. **Supply Diversification**
   - Fast-track approval of biosimilar/additional API suppliers (India, Mexico) to reduce USD dependency
   - Negotiate regional pricing agreements with WHO procurement partners (PAHO, AMRO)
   - Explore licensed importation from regional manufacturers (Mexico, Argentina) in local currency

5. **Financial Mechanisms**
   - Temporary budget reallocation within SGSSS oncology reserves
   - Establish exchange-rate hedging fund for critical chemotherapy agents (pilot with doxorubicin)
   - Negotiate payment term extensions with suppliers (Net 60/90) contingent on devaluation timeline

6. **Regulatory Streamlining**
   - Temporarily waive import requirements for pre-qualified manufacturers to accelerate sourcing alternatives
   - Permit emergency procurement across borders (regional sourcing) with expedited customs clearance

### Structural Resilience (3–12 months)
7. **Strategic Stockpiling**
   - Establish national oncology buffer stock (2–3 months) for Category A (non-substitutable) agents
   - Decentralize cold-chain infrastructure in 8–10 regional hubs
