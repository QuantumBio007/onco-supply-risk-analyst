# Drug Shortage Risk Brief: Doxorubicin in Colombia
## Currency Devaluation Scenario

---

## Drug Profile

**Generic Name:** Doxorubicin hydrochloride  
**Therapeutic Class:** Anthracycline chemotherapy agent  
**Formulations in Colombian Market:** IV solution (10 mg/mL, 50 mg/100 mg vials)  
**Clinical Indications:** Breast cancer, lymphomas, sarcomas, leukemias  
**Current Colombian Demand:** Estimated 800–1,200 treatment cycles/month across public and private sectors  

**Key Characteristics:**
- Essential oncology drug (WHO Essential Medicines List)
- No widely accepted therapeutic alternatives for certain indications
- Room-temperature stability allows 60–90 day supply buffers
- Approximately 60–70% of Colombian supply sourced from international manufacturers (India, China, Europe)

---

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

### Import Dependency & Currency Exposure
- **Import reliance:** 65–75% of doxorubicin supply requires USD/EUR payments
- **Primary suppliers:** Pfizer (multinational), Teva (Israel), Cipla/Cadila (India), domestic generic manufacturers (Tecnoquímicas, Laboratorios Andrómaco)
- **Currency risk:** Colombian Peso (COP) historically volatile; 2022–2023 devaluation episodes saw 18–25% depreciation against USD

### Margin Compression Mechanism
- Local manufacturers operating on 15–25% gross margins face immediate cost pressures
- Importers holding USD-denominated payables experience 20–30% unbudgeted cost increases
- Public sector price-fixing mechanisms (INVIMA, MINSALUD) prevent rapid price adjustment, delaying margin recovery

### Supply Chain Fragility Indicators
| Factor | Vulnerability Level |
|--------|---------------------|
| Working capital constraints in local firms | High |
| Import advance payment requirements (60–90 days) | High |
| Stock-out recovery time (production restart) | 45–60 days |
| Alternative domestic manufacturing capacity | Low |
| Strategic inventory reserves | Minimal (<20 days) |

### Institutional Bottlenecks
- INVIMA drug approval timelines: 30–60 days for new suppliers
- Hospital procurement cycles locked 3–6 months in advance
- Limited price negotiation flexibility in public tenders due to budget caps
- Distribution network consolidation (3 major wholesalers control ~60% of market)

---

## Scenario Impact Analysis

### Phase 1: Immediate (Weeks 1–4)
**COP devaluation of 15–20% against USD**

- **Importer behavior:** Suppliers reduce import volumes by 30–40% to preserve cash flow; orders shift to slower, cheaper ocean freight (30-day delay vs. 7-day air freight)
- **Domestic manufacturer strain:** Local producers with USD-denominated API (active pharmaceutical ingredient) contracts face sudden 15–20% input cost increases
- **Market signal:** Spot prices for doxorubicin increase 8–12% in private sector; public sector prices remain frozen
- **Stock status:** No immediate shortage; existing inventory at distributors (15–20 days) and hospitals (5–10 days) remains stable

### Phase 2: Medium-term (Weeks 5–12)
**Persistent devaluation; suppliers adjust supply commitments**

- **Importer exit:** Smaller importers (2–3 firms) suspend operations or reduce SKU portfolios; concentrate purchasing power in 2–3 dominant suppliers
- **Public sector impact:** INVIMA price-capped purchases fall below equilibrium; suppliers prioritize private-sector, non-capped sales; public hospital procurement falls 25–35%
- **Domestic production:** Tecnoquímicas, Andrómaco increase domestic API sourcing attempts but face 8–12 week lead times; no immediate capacity relief
- **Stock status:** Hospital inventory depletes from 10 days to 3–5 days; regional variation emerges (Bogotá/Medellín stable, peripheral departments at risk)
- **Shortage probability:** 35–45% risk of localized stockouts in smaller public hospitals

### Phase 3: Sustained Impact (Weeks 13+)
**Currency stabilization uncertain; structural supply contraction**

- **Market restructuring:** 1–2 smaller importers exit; supplier base consolidates to 4–5 firms (vs. 8–10 pre-devaluation)
- **Price tier divergence:** Private sector prices 25–40% higher than public-capped prices; arbitrage and gray-market leakage possible
- **Public sector crisis:** If devaluation persists and budgets not adjusted, 40–50% shortage probability in public oncology units
- **Clinical impact:** Treatment delays 1–3 weeks; potential protocol deviations (dose reductions, extended intervals)
- **Domestic capacity:** New production lines (if launched) reach operational status in 6–9 months; insufficient to offset import gap

## Policy Recommendations

### For Ministry of Health (MINSALUD) & INVIMA

1. **Dynamic price adjustment mechanism**
   - Establish currency-indexed pricing formula for public procurement; allow quarterly adjustments tied to COP/USD exchange rate movements (trigger: >8% monthly change)
   - Prevents supplier margin collapse and maintains import incentives
   - Estimated impact: Reduces shortage risk by 30–40%

2. **Strategic inventory buffer**
   - Build 30-day national oncology drug reserve for critical agents (doxorubicin, cisplatin, 5-FU)
