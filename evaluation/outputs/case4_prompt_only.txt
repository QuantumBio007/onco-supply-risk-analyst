# Drug Shortage Risk Brief: Doxorubicin in Colombia
## Currency Devaluation Scenario

---

## Drug Profile

**Drug:** Doxorubicin (doxorubicin hydrochloride)
- **Therapeutic class:** Anthracycline chemotherapy agent
- **Primary indications:** Breast cancer, lymphomas, sarcomas, multiple myeloma
- **Formulations in Colombian market:** IV solution (10 mg/mL), lyophilized powder
- **Annual Colombian consumption estimate:** 800–1,200 kg active pharmaceutical ingredient (API) equivalent
- **Manufacturer concentration:** 60% imported from North America/Europe; 40% regional manufacturing (Mexico, Argentina)

---

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

### Current State Assessment

| Vulnerability Factor | Risk Level | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Import dependency** | HIGH | Doxorubicin API primarily sourced from USA, Germany, Switzerland; finished goods from multiple origins |
| **Currency exposure** | CRITICAL | Colombian peso (COP) historically volatile; 2023–2024 devaluation cycles averaged 8–15% annually |
| **Pricing mechanism** | HIGH | Importers quote prices in USD; 30–45 day payment terms expose Colombian healthcare buyers to exchange rate swings |
| **Inventory buffers** | MODERATE | Hospital networks maintain 2–4 month stock; some regional wholesalers operate on 45–60 day cycles |
| **Regulatory approval** | LOW | Doxorubicin established; no additional registrations needed for existing suppliers |

### Currency Devaluation Cascade Effects

**Direct cost impact:**
- **Scenario assumption:** 20% COP devaluation vs. USD over 6 months
- **Import cost increase:** 20–25% for USD-denominated purchases
- **Example:** 100 mg vial currently ~COP 180,000 (USD ~$45) → COP 216,000–225,000 effective cost

**Secondary disruptions:**
1. **Procurement stalling:** Hospital purchasing committees may freeze orders awaiting price stabilization, delaying replenishment
2. **Supplier margin compression:** Local wholesalers operating on 12–18% margins face erosion; some may deprioritize doxorubicin for higher-margin products
3. **Regional arbitrage:** Neighboring countries (Venezuela, Ecuador, Panama) may absorb Colombian supplies if parallel market incentives activate
4. **Budget misalignment:** Institutional budgets locked in COP; mid-year currency shocks create funding gaps for Q3–Q4 procurement

## Scenario Impact Analysis

### Timeline & Severity Projection

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4): Early Warning**
- Importers announce price increases of 15–20%
- Hospital finance departments request budget reforecasts
- Severity: **Minimal shortage risk** (existing inventory sufficient)
- Affected populations: None yet

**Phase 2 (Weeks 5–12): Transition Stress**
- Some procurement orders cancelled or deferred by mid-tier hospitals
- Regional wholesaler inventory begins declining (normal turnover not fully replaced)
- Specialty cancer centers prioritize; public hospital networks deprioritize elective oncology programs
- Severity: **Moderate shortage risk** in non-major urban centers
- Affected populations: ~15–25% of non-tertiary oncology patients (estimated 8,000–12,000 patients) experience treatment delays of 2–6 weeks

**Phase 3 (Weeks 13–26): Systemic Strain**
- Inventory at wholesalers/hospitals drops below 6-week safety threshold
- Suppliers may allocate limited stock to high-volume buyers (tertiary centers)
- Informal/gray-market purchases increase 30–50%
- Severity: **High shortage risk** for underserved regions (Cauca, Nariño, Putumayo departments)
- Affected populations: ~20–35% of Colombian oncology patients (~15,000–25,000) experience partial or complete treatment interruptions

**Phase 4 (Weeks 27+): Potential Stabilization or Crisis**
- Scenario A (favorable): Currency stabilizes; importers restore normal order flow; shortage resolves in weeks 28–32
- Scenario B (unfavorable): Currency continues depreciating; Central Bank restricts foreign exchange; critical shortage emerges; treatment deferrals extend 8+ weeks
- Affected populations: Scenario B could affect 40%+ of non-private sector oncology patients

### Vulnerable Populations

- **Public hospital patients** (limited budget flexibility)
- **Rural/remote oncology programs** (lower purchasing power, longer supply chains)
- **Uninsured/underinsured populations** (price-sensitive)
- **Patients with triple-negative or aggressive breast cancers** (doxorubicin primary agent; limited substitutes)
