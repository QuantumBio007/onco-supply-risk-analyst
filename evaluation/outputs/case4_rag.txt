## Drug Profile

Doxorubicin is an anthracycline antibiotic chemotherapy agent used to treat breast cancer, leukemia, lymphoma, and sarcomas. It is on the WHO Model List of Essential Medicines (EML, oncology section). Conventional doxorubicin is fully off-patent and generic. India is the dominant global supplier of doxorubicin API, with a smaller contribution from China. Colombia has no domestic API manufacturing capacity and is fully import-dependent on India and China. Doxorubicin is available as injectable solution and powder for reconstitution, requiring refrigeration at 2–8°C and protection from light, making it vulnerable to logistics disruptions that threaten both product integrity and supply availability.

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

**Import dependency and API concentration:** Colombia relies entirely on Indian and Chinese API imports for doxorubicin. Global API supply is dominated by Indian manufacturers; a 2011–2012 manufacturing quality crisis at major Indian and Chinese suppliers caused widespread shortages that cascaded into Latin American markets. Subsequent shortages occurred in 2022–2023, again linked to API supply concentration.

**Regulatory gate:** Doxorubicin must have an active INVIMA (Instituto Nacional de Vigilancia de Medicamentos y Alimentos) sanitary registration before commercial distribution in Colombia. Alternative suppliers without INVIMA registration cannot rapidly substitute during supply disruptions. As of 2024, INVIMA maintained a backlog of over 14,000 pending registrations across all product categories, creating a regulatory bottleneck.

**Financial procurement failure:** Colombia's EPS-IPS-distributor system faces systemic financial fragility. Total EPS-IPS debt reached approximately COP 32.9 trillion in 2024 (up from COP 25 trillion in 2023). Approximately 80% of EPS lack legally required reserves. Distributors restrict drug shipments to EPS in arrears, creating de-facto shortages at point of care. This financial blockade persists despite government payment of 90.3% of pharmaceutical invoices in 2024, indicating intermediation friction as the root cause.

**Cold chain vulnerability:** Refrigeration requirements (2–8°C) mean that port delays, customs holds, or power outages directly threaten product integrity independent of supply availability.

**Key parameters:**
- Mean lead time during disruption: 28.0 days (std: 7.8 days)
- Effective order fill rate: 75% (reflecting 17% structural procurement failure rate)

- Disruption duration: ~180 days (geometric distribution)
- Optimal reorder point: 237 units; Economic Order Quantity: 241 units

**Interpretation:** The 8.9 stockout days per year falls within acceptable operational range under current modeling assumptions. However, this baseline assumes Colombia's 80% probability of experiencing any stockout during the year persists. The 48% budget multiplier directly reflects currency devaluation's impact on hospital procurement budgets denominated in Colombian pesos, reducing effective order quantities. The 75% fill rate reflects chronic structural procurement failures (~17% order failure or severe shortfall due to fiscal constraints), not scenario-specific supply constraints.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Establish INVIMA fast-track pathway:** Expedite sanitary registration for secondary doxorubicin suppliers (particularly Chinese manufacturers) to reduce regulatory bottleneck during supply disruptions. Current 14,000+ registration backlog must be cleared or triaged by product criticality.

2. **Currency-hedged procurement contracts:** EPS and hospital networks should negotiate fixed-USD pricing with distributors or establish currency hedge agreements to decouple procurement from peso depreciation shocks, preserving effective budget purchasing power.

3. **Central strategic reserve:** MINSALUD should establish a 30–60 day centralized buffer stock of doxorubicin (conventional formulation) managed through SISCAATS (the new integrated shortage monitoring system under development). This reserve should be pre-positioned in climate-controlled regional distribution hubs to protect against simultaneous cold-chain and financial disruption.

4. **EPS financial restructuring:** Address the root cause of de-facto shortages—the EPS debt cascade (COP 32.9 trillion in 2024)—through mandatory financial reconciliation and payment cycle compression. The fact that government paid 90.3% of invoices in 2024 yet supply failures persisted confirms that intermediation friction, not absolute funding, is the bottleneck.

5. **Tutela volume monitoring:** Approximately 4,400 medication-specific tutelas were filed in the first 8 months of 2025 (per Defensoría del Pueblo accompaniment data). Track doxorubicin-specific tutelas as a real-time shortage signal independent of INVIMA's formal shortage classification, since tutela volume often precedes official shortage declaration.

6. **API diversification:** Work with MINSALUD and pharmaceutical importers to evaluate feasibility of API sourcing from secondary suppliers (e.g., diversification within India's second-tier producers or evaluation of South Korean or European API sources) to reduce concentration risk in three to five primary Indian manufacturers.

## Confidence & Limitations

**Confidence:**
- The currency devaluation scenario parameters (60% budget compression scenario factor, 48% effective budget multiplier) are grounded in the retrieved modeling context.
- Stockout days (8.9 ± 0.7 days/year) are derived from 500 Monte Carlo simulations using established inventory theory (Warren OM textbook continuous-review (Q, r) model).
- The 17% structural procurement failure rate in Colombia is documented in the retrieved context as "chronic structural procurement failures (~17% of orders fail or arrive severely short)."
- The EPS debt cascade and INVIMA regulatory backlog are documented as of 2024–2025.

**Limitations:**
- The stockout model is illustrative, not actuarial. Real-world doxorubicin stockout duration and frequency depend on granular hospital-level procurement data, which is not fully transparent in Colombia's fragmented EPS-IPS-distributor system.
- The 75% fill rate assumption reflects "structural floor 83% × scenario 90%" but the derivation of these floor and scenario multipliers is not fully traced in the retrieved context.
- The model does not capture the risk that currency devaluation may trigger simultaneous balance-of-payments constraints that force import restrictions (not just budget compression), which could sharply increase lead times beyond the modeled 28.0-day mean.
- INVIMA registration backlog (14,000+ pending) is noted as of 2024, but the specific breakdown by oncology products is not provided. Actual regulatory unblocking speed is uncertain.
- Tutela volume (4,400 medication tutelas in 8 months of 2025) is a proxy for real-world access failure but does not directly measure doxorubicin stockout frequency. Tutelas are filed for multiple reasons (denial, delay, unavailability); the proportion attributable to supply shortage vs. financial denial is not isolated in the retrieved data.
- The cold-chain integrity risk (refrigeration disruption) is qualitatively noted but not quantified in the modeling. A power outage or logistics delay in ports could compromise doxorubicin batches without triggering a counted "stockout day" if physical units remain on shelves but are unusable.
