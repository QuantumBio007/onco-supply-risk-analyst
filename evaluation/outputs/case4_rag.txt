## Drug Profile

Doxorubicin is an anthracycline chemotherapy agent on the WHO Model List of Essential Medicines. It is generic and off-patent, with active pharmaceutical ingredient (API) manufacturing concentrated in India and China. Global doxorubicin supply has experienced multiple documented shortages: widespread shortages in 2011–2012 due to manufacturing quality issues at major producers, and again in 2022–2023 linked to API supply concentration and manufacturing consolidation. These global disruptions cascade into Latin American markets because the same API suppliers serve both regions.

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

Colombia's oncology drug procurement operates through two mandatory health insurance regimes (contributory and subsidized) administered by EPS entities (health insurers), which negotiate procurement contracts with distributors and manufacturers. All doxorubicin must hold valid INVIMA registration before legal marketing.

Colombia is fully import-dependent for doxorubicin; there is no domestic API manufacturing fallback. Doxorubicin is traded globally in USD, while Colombian EPS procurement budgets are peso-denominated. Peso depreciation directly reduces purchasing power for imported drugs. The context notes that peso devaluation impact is "less acute than in Argentina but still a material factor in procurement planning." Under currency devaluation, the effective procurement budget is compressed to approximately 51% of baseline (structural cap 85% × scenario impact 60%), reducing order volumes and increasing stockout risk—particularly acute for public hospital systems and EPS entities with fixed peso budgets.

## Scenario Impact Analysis

**Currency Devaluation Scenario (Peso devaluation, budget compression):**

A Monte Carlo inventory simulation (500 runs, 365 days) using a continuous-review (Q, r) model with stochastic lead times projects:

- **Stockout days per year:** 4.2 days ± 0.48 (95% CI)
- **Service level (unit fill rate):** 99.0% ± 0.0%
- **Service level (days without stockout):** 98.9%
- **Probability of any stockout during the year:** 59%
- **Probability of critical shortage (>60 stockout days):** 0.0%
- **Mean lead time:** 28.0 days (std: 5.6 days)
- **Effective order fill rate:** 84%
- **Average inventory on hand:** 131 units

**Interpretation:** Under isolated currency devaluation conditions, doxorubicin supply in Colombia is projected to experience minimal disruption (4.2 stockout days/year), with service levels exceeding 99%. This falls within acceptable operational range. However, the simulation assumes normal supplier performance and a 28-day lead time; actual disruption would depend on EPS procurement response speed and budget reallocation mechanisms.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Monitor EPS budget execution** during peso devaluation episodes. Establish early-warning protocols to detect when procurement budgets fall below levels needed to maintain safety stock (56 units per optimal policy).

2. **Coordinate procurement across EPS entities** through group purchasing arrangements to consolidate orders and maintain negotiating leverage with suppliers during currency stress periods.

3. **Develop contingency authorization pathways** for temporary INVIMA importation of doxorubicin from pre-qualified international suppliers if domestic channels face acute delays, to avoid regulatory gridlock during supply disruption.

4. **Establish national-level inventory visibility mechanism** (distinct from regulatory traceability) to enable real-time tracking of available doxorubicin stock across public hospitals and major EPS facilities, enabling rapid reallocation during localized shortages.

5. **Index high-cost oncology procurement budgets** to exchange rate baskets or forward-cover a portion of USD denominated drug purchases to reduce procurement volatility during depreciation episodes.

## Confidence & Limitations

**Confidence:**
- Simulation results are mathematically sound within the stated model assumptions (continuous-review inventory, log-normal lead time distribution, geometric disruption duration).
- Doxorubicin's history of global shortages (2011–2012, 2022–2023) is documented in the retrieved context.
- Colombia's peso depreciation impact and EPS budget structure are accurately characterized.

**Limitations:**
- The simulation assumes a 28-day lead time during disruption; actual lead times depend on supplier allocation priority and are unobserved for Colombia.
- The 84% effective order fill rate is assumed based on "structural performance under current market conditions" but lacks documented baseline data for Colombia-specific supplier behavior.
- The budget multiplier (51%) is modeled but not validated against actual EPS purchasing data during recent depreciation episodes.
- **Critical gap:** The context explicitly states this analysis "requires validation from a Colombia-based healthcare professional or supply chain expert before Week 8 final evaluation." Key unknowns include: actual EPS procurement coordination mechanisms in practice, INVIMA registration timelines for generic doxorubicin formulations, and documented shortage events in Colombia during 2023–2025.
- The simulation does not account for substitution dynamics (whether oncologists shift to alternative anthracyclines during shortage) or treatment deferral, which would alter effective demand.
- No data on current doxorubicin inventory levels, typical EPS ordering patterns, or supplier contracts in Colombia are available in the retrieved context.

**Baseline currency devaluation alone does not trigger critical shortage risk**, but combined with API supply restrictions (documented in a separate scenario) risk increases substantially. See the combined shock analysis for context.
