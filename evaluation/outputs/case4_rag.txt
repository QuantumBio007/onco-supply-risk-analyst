## Drug Profile

Doxorubicin is an anthracycline antibiotic chemotherapy agent on the WHO Model List of Essential Medicines (EML). It is used to treat breast cancer, leukemia, lymphoma, and sarcomas, and is one of the most broadly used oncology drugs globally. Conventional doxorubicin is fully off-patent and generic.

API manufacturing is concentrated in India (dominant global supplier) and China (secondary source). Colombia has no domestic API manufacturing capacity and is entirely import-dependent on Indian and Chinese suppliers. Doxorubicin is available as an injectable solution and powder for reconstitution, requiring refrigeration at 2–8°C and protection from light. This cold-chain requirement means logistics disruptions — port delays, customs holds, power outages — directly threaten product integrity in addition to supply availability.

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

**Regulatory bottleneck:** Doxorubicin must have an active INVIMA (Instituto Nacional de Vigilancia de Medicamentos y Alimentos) sanitary registration before legal distribution in Colombia. As of 2024, INVIMA maintains a backlog of over 14,000 pending registrations across all product categories. During supply disruptions, alternative suppliers without INVIMA registration cannot rapidly substitute, eliminating a critical mitigation pathway.

**Import dependence and currency exposure:** Colombia procures doxorubicin entirely from international suppliers, with pricing denominated in USD. Hospital procurement budgets are denominated in Colombian pesos. Currency devaluation directly erodes purchasing power: EPS (health insurers) and IPS (hospital networks) experience reduced order volumes and increased likelihood of deferred purchases and stockouts.

**Structural procurement fragmentation:** The EPS-IPS-distributor pharmaceutical debt cascade creates a de facto financial blockade independent of physical supply availability. As of July 2025, EPS-IPS debt reached COP 32.9 trillion, with the pharmaceutical component at COP 4.2 trillion. Approximately 80% of EPS lack legally required financial reserves. Payment cycles extend 180–365+ days in practice, and distributors (Audifarma, Cruz Verde, Cafam, etc.) restrict shipments to entities in arrears. This fragmentation reduces visibility of aggregate national stock levels.

**API supply concentration:** Global doxorubicin shortages in 2011–2012 and 2022–2023 were linked to manufacturing quality issues and consolidation among Indian and Chinese API producers. Colombia, as part of the Latin American import-dependent cohort, is exposed to the same API supply shocks that affect developed markets.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Establish a national strategic reserve for doxorubicin:** Procure and maintain a 60–90-day buffer stock (approximately 900–1,350 units based on average inventory modeling) held at a centralized cold-chain facility under MINSALUD or a designated gestión centralizada. This buffer decouples procurement timing from currency fluctuations and distributor payment cycles.

2. **Implement INVIMA registration acceleration pathway:** Develop an expedited registration track for doxorubicin suppliers currently approved in WHO-recognized regulatory systems (FDA, EMA, Health Canada) to reduce regulatory bottleneck during supply disruptions. Target: approve alternative suppliers within 30 days rather than months.

3. **Integrate doxorubicin into SISCAATS early:** Ensure doxorubicin is prioritized in MINSALUD's new Sistema de Información para la Centralización de Alertas de Abastecimiento (SISCAATS) monitoring system. Real-time visibility of EPS-IPS stock levels will enable early detection of procurement friction and allow corrective action before patient-level shortages occur.

4. **Restructure EPS payment timelines for oncology drugs:** Amend UPC capitation and presupuestos máximos administration to guarantee payment to pharmaceutical distributors within 30 days for PBS-covered oncology drugs (including doxorubicin), decoupling supplier risk from EPS financial solvency. Consider escrow or automated clearing house (ACH) settlement.

5. **Hedge currency exposure in procurement contracts:** Negotiate USD-denominated purchase commitments with quarterly price adjustments tied to official exchange rates, with a contractual cap on peso devaluation pass-through (e.g., limit to 80% of devaluation impact) to stabilize budget predictability.

6. **Coordinate cross-border procurement data:** Establish information-sharing agreements with Argentina and Ecuador on doxorubicin availability and lead times from shared Indian and Chinese suppliers. Aggregate demand signals may improve negotiating leverage and early-warning capability.

## Confidence & Limitations

**Confidence:**
- The WHO EML status for doxorubicin is confirmed in the retrieved context.
- India and China as dominant API sources are explicitly documented.
- Colombia's 100% import dependence and lack of domestic API manufacturing are confirmed.
- The regulatory role of INVIMA and the existence of a registration backlog are documented.
- The EPS-IPS debt cascade and its magnitude (COP 32.9 trillion as of July 2025) are sourced from MINSALUD official reporting.

**Limitations:**
- **Stockout projections are model-based estimates, not historical actuals.** The Monte Carlo simulation (500 runs, 365-day horizon) is illustrative and depends heavily on assumed demand distributions (normal, truncated), fill rates (75%), and lead-time parameters (28 days mean). Real-world variability may differ substantially.
- **The 75% fill rate assumption reflects "chronic structural procurement failures (~17% order failure rate)" but does not isolate the currency devaluation scenario effect from baseline fragmentation.** The modeled budget multiplier of 48% is a scenario-specific allocation, not an empirically validated elasticity for Colombian hospital purchasing under peso devaluation.
- **Cold-chain integrity is not quantified.** Refrigeration failures, storage losses, and logistics delays during disruptions are acknowledged as threats but are not incorporated into the stockout model. Actual shortage duration and severity may be underestimated.
- **Tumor burden and patient-level impact are outside the scope of this brief.** Supply risk is measured in inventory days and service level; clinical consequences (treatment delay, disease progression) are not assessed.
- **No proprietary procurement data from Colombian EPS, IPS, or distributors are included.** Visibility is limited to published MINSALUD, SuperSalud, and Defensoría del Pueblo reporting. Actual inventory positions and order patterns are not directly observed.
- **SISCAATS is a proposed system not yet fully implemented.** The recommendation to integrate doxorubicin early assumes enactment and operational capability by late 2025–2026.
