# Drug Shortage Risk Brief: Cisplatin in Argentina
## Baseline Scenario

---

## Drug Profile

**Generic Name:** Cisplatin (cis-diamminedichloroplatinum II)

**Therapeutic Class:** Platinum-based chemotherapy agent

**Key Oncology Indications:** Testicular cancer, ovarian cancer, bladder cancer, head and neck cancers, lung cancer (component of combination regimens)

**Formulation & Strength:** 10 mg/50 mg vials (intravenous solution)

**Regulatory Status in Argentina:** Approved by ANMAT (Administración Nacional de Medicamentos, Alimentos y Tecnología Médica); listed on essential medicines formulary

**Market Position:** Off-patent; multiple generic manufacturers operate in Latin America and globally

---

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

**Current Baseline Supply Posture: LOW-MODERATE RISK**

### Manufacturing & Sourcing
- **Primary Sources for Argentina:** Domestic generic manufacturers (limited capacity) + regional imports (primarily Brazil, Mexico, Chile)
- **Active Domestic Producers:** 2–3 Argentine pharmaceutical companies with cisplatin manufacturing capability; limited to API formulation/packaging
- **Bulk API Supply Chain:** Heavy reliance on imports from India and China (commodity chemical suppliers); 8–12 week lead times typical
- **Geographic Concentration Risk:** Moderate—India dominates global cisplatin API production (>40% global capacity); single-source dependency for some manufacturers

### Distribution Infrastructure
- **Hospital Procurement:** Centralized purchasing through public hospital networks (HIBA, Hospital Garrahan, provincial facilities)
- **Private Sector Access:** Distributed via licensed pharmaceutical wholesalers; adequate cold-chain logistics in Buenos Aires metropolitan area
- **Rural/Remote Access:** Vulnerable in provinces with limited oncology centers; reliance on inter-provincial redistribution

### Regulatory & Market Factors
- **Price Controls:** Argentina implements price regulation on essential oncology drugs; may reduce manufacturer profit margins and incentivize production deferral during inflationary periods
- **Currency Volatility:** Argentine peso depreciation increases import costs; manufacturers may delay restocking during macroeconomic instability
- **Antitrust/Competition:** Multiple domestic players provide baseline competition, reducing monopoly bottleneck risk

### Demand Patterns
- **Oncology Patient Burden:** ~13,000 new cancer cases annually in Argentina; platinum-based regimens account for ~25–30% of curative-intent chemotherapy
- **Consumption Stability:** Relatively stable year-round with seasonal variation (minor)

## Scenario Impact Analysis

### Baseline Conditions (No Disruption)
Under normal operating conditions without exogenous shocks:

| Parameter | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| **Supply-Demand Balance** | Adequate; domestic + regional imports cover ~95% of demand |
| **Days of Stock (Public System)** | 30–45 days typical; fluctuates seasonally |
| **Hospital Access (Buenos Aires)** | Reliable (>95% of cancer centers) |
| **Hospital Access (Provinces)** | Moderate (70–85%); intermittent delays 1–4 weeks |
| **Average Lead Time (Restocking)** | 6–8 weeks for domestic; 8–12 weeks for imports |
| **Price Volatility** | Stable (regulated); minor fluctuations ±5% annually |

### Clinical Consequence Indicators
- **Therapy Deferral Risk:** Low (non-substitutable in many protocols, but some regimen alternatives exist for palliative settings)
- **Treatment Delay Impact:** Moderate—delays >2 weeks may compromise cure rates in curative-intent testicular/ovarian cancer protocols
- **Vulnerable Populations:** Pediatric oncology centers (smaller patient populations, lower stockpile thresholds) and provincial public hospitals (limited purchasing power)

## Policy Recommendations

### Near-Term (0–6 months)

1. **Inventory Surveillance**
   - Establish baseline cisplatin stock monitoring across public hospital oncology units via ANMAT reporting portal
   - Set minimum strategic reserve threshold: 60 days consumption equivalent for major oncology centers
   - Deploy quarterly reporting from domestic manufacturers on API import schedules and inventory levels

2. **Supply Chain Visibility**
   - Map cisplatin procurement pathways for top 10 cancer centers and provincial hospitals
   - Identify alternative regional suppliers (Brazil, Mexico, Chile) and formalize secondary source agreements
   - Document API supplier concentration risk (India/China) in pharmaceutical sector risk register

### Medium-Term (6–18 months)

3. **Domestic Capacity Strengthening**
   - Conduct feasibility assessment for increased domestic cisplatin API manufacturing or regional hub partnerships (e.g., with Brazilian producers)
   - Explore subsidized API procurement agreements for domestic manufacturers to reduce cost-driven import delays
   - Establish emergency protocol for provincial hospitals to access central stockpiles during shortages

4. **Regulatory Mechanism**
   - Formalize cisplatin shortage alert protocol with ANMAT; require 30-day advance notification from manufacturers of supply disruptions
   - Introduce manufacturer penalties for unjustified stock depletion (price controls framework should permit quality-based exceptions)

### Long-Term (18+ months)

5. **Regional Collaboration**
   - Negotiate bilateral agreements with Brazil and Chile for mutual cisplatin supply solidarity during regional disruptions
   - Support development of regional API manufacturing hub (MERCOSUR initiative) to reduce extra-regional dependency

6. **Demand Optimization**
   - Promote evidence-based protocol harmonization across provinces to reduce wastage and enable demand forecasting
   - Develop cisplatin conservation guidelines (e.g., dose rounding, protocol optimization) in consultation with oncology societies
