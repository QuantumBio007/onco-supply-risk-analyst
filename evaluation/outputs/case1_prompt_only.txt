# Drug Shortage Risk Brief: Cisplatin in Argentina
## Baseline Scenario

---

## Drug Profile

**Drug:** Cisplatin (Cis-diamminedichloroplatinum II)

**Therapeutic Class:** Platinum-based chemotherapy agent

**Key Uses in Oncology:**
- First-line treatment for testicular cancer
- Head and neck squamous cell carcinoma
- Bladder cancer
- Ovarian cancer
- Lung cancer (non-small cell and small cell)
- Gastric and esophageal cancers

**Formulation & Dosing:**
- Available as IV solution: 1 mg/mL concentrations
- Typical dosing: 50–100 mg/m² per cycle
- Standard cycle: 21–28 days

**Market Status in Argentina:**
- Essential medicine on ANMAT (National Administration of Medicine, Food and Medical Technology) formulary
- Generic formulations available
- Estimated annual consumption: 8,000–12,000 treatment cycles across public and private sectors

---

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

### **Current Supply Structure**

| Component | Status | Risk Level |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Primary Sources** | Domestic generic manufacturers (3–4 active); regional imports (Chile, Brazil) | Low–Moderate |
| **Manufacturing Capacity** | Limited domestic API (active pharmaceutical ingredient) production; ~60% import-dependent | Moderate |
| **Distribution** | ANMAT-regulated; public procurement via PAMI & provincial health systems | Low |
| **Regulatory Pathway** | Registration fast-track for oncology drugs; minimal approval delays | Low |

### **Vulnerability Factors (Baseline)**

1. **API Sourcing Dependency**
   - Platinum salts primarily sourced from India, China, and Russia
   - No domestic platinum refining capacity
   - Geopolitical disruptions have minimal current impact

2. **Manufacturing Fragility**
   - Single-site production by 2–3 major domestic suppliers
   - Lyophilization capacity constraints during peak demand periods
   - Limited surge production capability

3. **Financial Constraints**
   - Public sector procurement budgets stable but tight
   - Generic price compression reduces manufacturer margins (~8–12%)
   - Private sector demand is price-sensitive

4. **Regulatory & Quality Assurance**
   - ANMAT inspection cycle: 18–24 months
   - Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) compliance generally adequate
   - No current quality recalls affecting major suppliers

5. **Demand Volatility**
   - Seasonal fluctuations in cancer diagnoses
   - Competing demand from neighboring countries (Uruguay, Paraguay)
   - Off-label usage in some regional hospitals increases unpredictability

### **Historical Shortage Events**
- 2019–2020: Brief 6-week shortage (2–3 weeks supply) due to supplier transition
- 2021: Isolated regional gaps in northern provinces (non-critical)
- **Baseline assumption:** No active shortage as of 2024

## Scenario Impact Analysis

### **Baseline Scenario Parameters**
- **Assumption:** Current geopolitical, economic, and regulatory conditions persist
- **Timeframe:** 12-month projection
- **Disruption Threshold:** >2 weeks supply chain delay = regional impact

### **Baseline Risk Assessment**

| Risk Factor | Likelihood | Potential Impact | Mitigation Status |
|-------------|------------|------------------|-------------------|
| **API Supply Disruption** | Low (5–10%) | Moderate (2–4 week delay) | Supplier diversification ongoing |
| **Manufacturer GMP Non-Compliance** | Very Low (1–2%) | High (8–12 week shortage) | Regulatory monitoring active |
| **Demand Surge** | Low–Moderate (15–20%) | Low (short-term rationing) | Strategic reserves limited |
| **Currency/Import Volatility** | Moderate (30–40%) | Low–Moderate (cost inflation 10–20%) | PAMI price controls partially protective |
| **Procurement Budget Cuts** | Moderate (20–25%) | Moderate (supply rationing) | Federal vs. provincial tension |

### **Baseline Outcome (12-month horizon)**

**Shortage Probability:** 15–25%
- **Severity:** Mild (intermittent 1–3 week gaps in 1–2 provinces)
- **Duration:** If occurs, likely 2–4 weeks
- **Affected Population:** <5% of treatment-eligible patients

**Supply Continuity:** **Adequate with Minor Disruptions**
- Domestic + regional imports sufficient to meet baseline demand
- Public sector access maintained in major urban centers
- Private sector may experience brief cost inflation

## Policy Recommendations

### **Short-term (0–6 months)**

1. **Supply Chain Visibility**
   - Establish formal quarterly reporting by ANMAT-registered cisplatin manufacturers on:
     - API inventory levels (minimum 60-day buffer)
     - Production capacity utilization
     - Planned maintenance shutdowns
   - Create supplier alert protocol for imports >15% cost increases

2. **Demand Stabilization**
   - Strengthen provincial-level oncology registries to forecast treatment volumes
   - Coordinate between PAMI, provincial health ministries, and oncology societies to align procurement cycles
   - Reduce off-label usage through clinical practice guidelines

3. **Regulatory Streamlining**
   - Pre-qualify 1–2 additional API suppliers for emergency authorization pathway
   - Expedite registration of alternative formulations (e.g., cisplatin liposomal if appropriate for specific indications)

### **Medium-term (6–18 months)**

4. **Strategic Reserves & Buffer Stock**
   - Establish minimal 30-day emergency reserve maintained by federal health authority (Ministry of Health)
   - Cost: ~$40,000–60,000 USD annually (modest)
   - Governance: Clear trigger mechanism for reserve activation

5. **Manufacturing Resilience**
   - Provide technical assistance to domestic manufacturers for:
     - Second-sourcing of API contracts
     - Lyophilization equipment upgrades
     - Waste reduction/efficiency improvements
   - Target: Increase domestic production capacity by 20–30%
