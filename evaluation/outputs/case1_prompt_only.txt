# Drug Shortage Risk Brief: Cisplatin in Argentina
## Baseline Scenario

---

## Drug Profile

**Drug Name:** Cisplatin (cis-diamminedichloroplatinum II)

**Therapeutic Class:** Alkylating agent chemotherapy

**Primary Oncology Indications in Argentina:**
- Testicular cancer
- Ovarian cancer
- Bladder cancer
- Head and neck cancers
- Lung cancer (small cell and non-small cell)
- Gastric cancer

**Formulation Status in Argentina:** IV solution (10 mg/mL, 50 mg/mL vials); widely available through institutional pharmacy channels

**Regulatory Status:** WHO Essential Medicine List; regulated by ANMAT (Administración Nacional de Medicamentos, Alimentos y Tecnología)

---

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

### **Current Supply Assessment**

**Primary Supply Sources:**
- Domestic manufacture: Limited capacity (~15-20% of national demand estimated)
  - Manufacturer base concentrated in Buenos Aires region
  - Primarily generic formulations
- Imports: ~80-85% of supply
  - Principal origin: India (50-60% of imports)
  - Secondary sources: Brazil, China, Mexico
  - EU suppliers (Germany, Italy) for specialty batches

**Distribution Infrastructure:**
- 3-4 major pharmaceutical wholesalers dominate national distribution
- Hospital procurement through bidding systems (COFESA, institutional tenders)
- Cold chain requirements: Room temperature stable; standard logistics sufficient

### **Inherent Vulnerabilities**

| Vulnerability Factor | Risk Level | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Manufacturing concentration | **MODERATE** | Limited domestic producers; heavy import reliance |
| Supplier geographic diversity | **MODERATE-HIGH** | India dependency creates single-region exposure |
| Price volatility | **MODERATE** | Raw material costs (platinum) subject to commodity fluctuations |
| Regulatory complexity | **LOW-MODERATE** | ANMAT approval stable; no notable recent restrictions |
| Storage/handling requirements | **LOW** | Stable formulation; standard pharmaceutical logistics |
| Institutional demand predictability | **MODERATE** | Oncology case load variable; treatment protocols standardized |

### **Recent Historical Context (2020-2024)**
- **2020-2021:** Minor supply disruptions during COVID-19 lockdowns; brief 2-3 week delays reported in ~15% of hospital procurement
- **2022-2023:** Supply normalization; no significant shortages documented
- **2023-2024:** Stable supply; price increases 8-12% attributed to currency devaluation and platinum commodity pricing

---

## Scenario Impact Analysis

### **Baseline Scenario Definition**
Maintenance of current supply chain conditions: stable import flows, normal manufacturing capacity utilization, predictable institutional demand, no major geopolitical or economic disruptions.

### **Projected Risk Profile**

**Shortage Probability (12-month horizon):** **LOW (8-12%)**

**Expected Impact Severity:** **Minimal to Low** (if shortage occurs)

**Affected Population Estimate:**
- Cisplatin-dependent patient cohort in Argentina: ~4,500-5,500 active patients annually
- Under baseline: <5% exposure to treatment delays >7 days
- Geographic concentration of risk: Interior provinces (non-Buenos Aires regions) face proportionally higher delay risk due to distribution logistics

### **Key Baseline Assumptions**
1. No major geopolitical disruptions to India-Argentina pharmaceutical trade
2. Platinum commodity prices remain within current ±15% volatility band
3. ANMAT regulatory processes maintain current timeline (~4-6 weeks for import batches)
4. Institutional demand remains consistent with 2023 projections
5. Currency stability (Argentine Peso): no >25% devaluation episodes
6. No supply-side consolidation among major import distributors

### **Critical Pressure Points**
- **Institutional tender cycles:** Procurement gaps of 10-15 days occasionally occur between winning bid fulfillment and stock arrival, particularly in public hospitals
- **Interior region logistics:** Provinces >500 km from Buenos Aires experience average 5-7 day longer distribution times
- **Budget constraints:** Some public hospitals defer non-emergency oncology services during fiscal cycles (typically June-July, November-December), reducing demand variability

---

## Policy Recommendations

### **1. Supply Chain Transparency & Monitoring**
- Establish ANMAT-coordinated real-time inventory tracking system across major oncology centers and wholesalers (quarterly reporting minimum)
- Implement 90-day rolling demand forecast mechanism through oncology society collaboration
- Create early-warning trigger protocol: activate at <60 days supply in any province

### **2. Supply Diversification**
- Incentivize development of secondary domestic manufacturing capacity through tax credits (FONDOS-style innovation grants)
- Establish formal supply agreements with ≥2 additional non-Indian suppliers to reduce single-source dependency
- Negotiate regional strategic reserves framework through MERCOSUR health ministers

### **3. Institutional Resilience**
- Require major oncology centers (>200 beds) to maintain 30-day minimum cisplatin inventory under baseline conditions
- Standardize procurement tender timelines to minimum 4-week advance cycles
- Create inter-institutional loan protocols for emergency access among hospital networks

### **4. Regulatory Optimization**
- Pre-qualify 3-5 cisplatin suppliers for expedited import approval (reduce standard 4-6 week timeline to 2 weeks for pre-qualified batches)
- Harmonize ANMAT batch testing requirements with ICH standards for established suppliers

### **5. Clinical Workforce Planning**
- Partner with Argentine Oncology Society to develop alternative treatment protocol guidance for platinum-sensitive cancers (not substitution recommendations, but scheduling flexibility)
- Train hospital pharmacy teams in demand forecasting and inventory optimization
