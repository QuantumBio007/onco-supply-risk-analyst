# DRUG SHORTAGE RISK BRIEF: CISPLATIN IN ARGENTINA
## Baseline Scenario Analysis

---

## Drug Profile

**Drug:** Cisplatin (cis-diamminedichloroplatinum II)
**Indication Focus:** Oncology (testicular, ovarian, bladder, lung, head & neck cancers)
**Formulation:** Intravenous solution (10 mg/mL, 50 mg vials standard)
**WHO Classification:** Essential Medicine (List inclusion: cancer chemotherapy)
**Argentina Market Status:** Approved; registered with ANMAT (Administración Nacional de Medicamentos, Alimentos y Tecnología Médica)

**Global Manufacturing Footprint:**
- Primary manufacturers: Teva (Israel), Hospira/Pfizer (USA, EU), Hikma (Jordan), Actavis (India)
- Limited Argentine domestic manufacturing (primarily formulation/repackaging; active pharmaceutical ingredients imported)
- Typical shelf life: 24–36 months; requires cool storage (2–8°C or ambient per formulation)

### Baseline Vulnerability Index: **MODERATE** (6/10)

**Key Drivers:**
1. **Import reliance:** 95% of cisplatin supply enters Argentina via import; no domestic API manufacturing
2. **Distributed procurement:** Fragmented purchasing across provincial health systems, PAMI, and private sector reduces coordination
3. **Cold chain gaps:** Provincial hospitals outside Buenos Aires/Córdoba may have inadequate refrigeration or stock tracking
4. **Currency exposure:** Argentine peso volatility (historical devaluation cycles) affects import costs and procurement budgets
5. **Price sensitivity:** Government price caps under baseline conditions may limit distributor incentive to maintain safety stock

## Policy Recommendations

### 1. **Supply Chain Visibility & Coordination**
- Establish a **National Cisplatin Inventory Dashboard** (ANMAT/Ministry of Health) tracking stock levels in real time across public and private sectors
- Mandate quarterly reporting by distributors and major hospitals
- **Timeline:** 6–12 months to implement
- **Ownership:** Ministry of Health, ANMAT

### 2. **Strategic Reserve Buffer**
- Create a **government-held emergency stock** (3–6 month supply equivalent for ~12,000 annual patients)
- Store in central location (Buenos Aires) with cold chain redundancy
- Activate only if commercial supply falls below 4-week threshold for 2+ consecutive weeks
- **Cost estimate:** ~USD 150,000–200,000 (procurement); ~USD 20,000/year (storage, rotation)

### 3. **Distributorship & Procurement Robustness**
- Encourage **dual-sourcing agreements** with manufacturers (e.g., Teva + Pfizer + regional supplier such as Hikma/Actavis)
- Provincial health systems should establish **minimum 8-week stock targets** for cisplatin
- Annual procurement planning (vs. ad-hoc) to stabilize pricing and ensure continuity

### 4. **Cold Chain Infrastructure**
- Audit provincial hospital refrigeration capacity; upgrade non-compliant sites
- Establish **temperature monitoring systems** (data loggers) on all distributed shipments
- Train pharmacy staff on proper storage/handling
- **Timeline:** 12–18 months; estimated cost ~USD 80,000–120,000

### 5. **Price & Reimbursement Stability**
- Review price-cap mechanisms to ensure distributors maintain adequate margins for safety stock
- Consider **tiered pricing** (government reference price for public procurement; market rates for private sector) to incentivize stable supply while protecting public budgets
