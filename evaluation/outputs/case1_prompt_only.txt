# Drug Shortage Risk Brief: Cisplatin in Argentina
## Baseline Scenario

---

## Drug Profile

**Drug Name:** Cisplatin (cis-diamminedichloroplatinum II)

**Therapeutic Class:** Platinum-based chemotherapy agent

**Primary Indications:** Testicular cancer, ovarian cancer, bladder cancer, lung cancer, head and neck cancers

**Formulation:** Intravenous solution (typically 1 mg/mL concentrations)

**Argentina Market Characteristics:**
- Essential oncology agent listed on Argentine Ministry of Health formularies
- Estimated annual consumption: 80-120 kg active pharmaceutical ingredient (API)
- Primary patient population: ~2,500-3,200 patients annually across public and private sectors
- Average treatment cost impact: Significant budget line for provincial health systems

---

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

**Current Supply Status (Baseline):**
- **Sourcing:** Argentina relies on imported API and finished pharmaceutical products from Europe, India, and China
- **Primary Suppliers:** 3-4 major pharmaceutical distributors; limited domestic manufacturing capacity
- **Local Production:** Minimal—Argentina imports ~85-90% of cisplatin needs as either API or finished product
- **Regulatory Pathway:** ANMAT (Administración Nacional de Medicamentos, Alimentos y Tecnología) approval required; registration process typically 18-24 months

**Vulnerability Factors (Baseline - Low-Moderate Risk):**

| Vulnerability Factor | Status | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|
| Supply concentration | 3-4 major importers | Moderate |
| Geographic diversification | EU, India, China sources | Low-Moderate |
| Strategic stockpiling | Limited public reserves | Moderate |
| Regulatory flexibility | Standard fast-track pathways exist | Low |
| Price volatility | Stable API costs (2021-2024) | Low |
| Transportation infrastructure | Buenos Aires port capacity adequate | Low |
| Cold chain requirements | Minimal (room temperature storage) | Low |

**Critical Chokepoints:**
1. **Import dependency:** No domestic synthesis capacity limits rapid response to supply disruptions
2. **Port throughput:** Buenos Aires port congestion can delay 2-3 week shipments by 5-7 days
3. **Currency exposure:** Argentine peso volatility affects import costs (potential 15-25% quarterly fluctuations)
4. **Regulatory approval delays:** New suppliers require ANMAT registration (18-24 months)

---

## Scenario Impact Analysis

**Baseline Scenario Definition:** 
Normal operating conditions with historical supply patterns, no major geopolitical disruptions, stable import pricing, and routine regulatory processing.

**Projected Shortage Risk: LOW (5-12% probability over 12-month horizon)**

**Impact Parameters Under Baseline:**

| Parameter | Projection |
|---|---|
| Supply adequacy | 95-100% of demand met |
| Lead time variability | ±2-3 weeks from order |
| Price fluctuation | ±5-8% annually |
| Patient access disruption | <2% of treatment courses delayed >1 week |
| Public sector impact | Minimal—adequate planning windows exist |

**Regional Vulnerability Gradient:**
- **Buenos Aires/CABA:** Lowest risk (direct port access, major hospital networks, multiple distribution hubs)
- **Provincial Centers (Córdoba, Rosario, Mendoza):** Low-moderate risk (7-10 day supply buffer, secondary distribution chains)
- **Interior Provinces:** Moderate risk (14-21 day supply chains, limited cold-storage alternatives)

**Patient Impact (Baseline):**
- Treatment initiation delays: <1% of scheduled oncology cases
- Dose modifications: Minimal (cisplatin dosing schedules allow 3-7 day windows)
- Protocol interruptions: 0-2 cases annually across public sector
- Equity concerns: Private sector maintains 2-3 month buffers; public sector 3-4 week buffers (manageable disparity)

**Health System Strain:**
- Oncology department operational impact: Negligible
- Alternative treatment availability: Limited—cisplatin is standard-of-care for several indications
- Workforce utilization: No significant disruption to scheduling

## Policy Recommendations

**Tier 1: Short-Term Stabilization (0-6 months)**

1. **Formalize Strategic Reserve**
   - Establish 8-week cisplatin buffer (300-400 kg API equivalent) across 3-4 regional nodes
   - Estimated cost: ARS 180-220 million (~USD 1.2-1.5M at baseline rates)
   - Assign responsibility: Federal Medicines Administration (AFIP) with provincial coordination

2. **Supplier Diversification Documentation**
   - Map all current API sources and finished product suppliers
   - Identify secondary suppliers in EU, India with capacity for surge production (10-15% volume increase)
   - Establish framework agreements with 2 additional pre-qualified importers

3. **Real-Time Monitoring System**
   - Implement basic supply tracking via ANMAT registration database
   - Monthly reporting from major distributors (sales volume, inventory, import schedules)
   - Automated alert threshold: <4-week national supply

**Tier 2: Medium-Term Resilience (6-18 months)**

4. **Regulatory Pathway Acceleration**
   - Pre-approve 2-3 additional cisplatin formulations from qualified suppliers
   - Reduce ANMAT review time from 18-24 months to 6-9 months via expedited pathway
   - Include European and Indian API manufacturers in advance qualification

5. **Demand Forecasting Enhancement**
   - Partner with provincial oncology networks to establish coordinated procurement
   - Develop 12-month rolling demand projections by tumor type and region
   - Integrate with national cancer registry (ROHA) for incidence-based planning

6. **Regional Distribution Optimization**
   - Upgrade secondary distribution hubs in Córdoba, Rosario, Mendoza
   - Establish minimum 2-week buffers at provincial nodes (additional ARS 80-120M investment)
   - Create emergency transport protocols for inter-provincial transfers

**Tier 3: Long-Term Structural Resilience (18+ months)**

7. **Domestic Capacity Development (Strategic Goal)**
