# CISPLATIN SHORTAGE RISK BRIEF: ARGENTINA
## Drug Shortage Scenario: API Export Restriction

---

## Drug Profile

**Drug:** Cisplatin (cis-diamminedichloroplatinum II)
**ATC Code:** L01XA01
**Formulation:** Injectable solution (1 mg/mL, 10 mg/mL, 50 mg/mL vials)
**Therapeutic Use:** First-line agent for testicular cancer, ovarian cancer, bladder cancer, and lung cancer treatment

**Current Argentine Market Position:**
- Essential cancer medicine on PAMI (pensioner drug coverage) and provincial formularies
- Annual estimated consumption: 8,000–12,000 treatment cycles
- Primary patient population: ~2,500–3,500 oncology patients annually requiring cisplatin-based regimens

**Supply Chain Characteristics:**
- No domestic API (active pharmaceutical ingredient) manufacturing in Argentina
- 100% reliance on imported bulk cisplatin or finished product
- Primary source countries: India, China, Russia, Japan
- Lead time from API order to patient administration: 90–120 days (including regulatory clearance)

---

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

### Current Sourcing Vulnerabilities

| Vulnerability Factor | Severity | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Single-source dependency** | **HIGH** | Argentina imports from limited number of WHO-GMP suppliers; disruption in any primary source creates immediate gap |
| **Strategic reserve absence** | **HIGH** | No national pharmaceutical buffer stock for oncology agents; hospitals maintain 30–60 day inventory only |
| **Regulatory lag** | **MEDIUM** | ANMAT (Administración Nacional de Medicamentos) approval for new suppliers requires 6–8 months |
| **Port infrastructure** | **MEDIUM** | Buenos Aires port congestion (8–12% of shipments experience 2-week delays); inland provinces face additional logistics friction |
| **Cold chain stability** | **LOW** | Cisplatin is relatively thermally stable; however, compounded vial forms require 2–8°C storage during distribution |

### Supplier Concentration

**Primary API Exporters (estimated market share):**
- India (Cipla, Fresenius Kabi, Teva): ~45% of Argentine supply
- China (Shanghai, Hangzhou manufacturers): ~35%
- Japan/Europe: ~20%

**If India implements API export restriction:** Would trigger immediate loss of 40–45% of Argentine cisplatin supply within 4–6 weeks (time for inventory depletion at current usage rates).

---

## Scenario Impact Analysis

### API Export Restriction Mechanics
**Assumption:** India (primary source) implements temporary or permanent cisplatin API export ban due to:
- Domestic supply emergency (local cancer patient surge)
- Government directive to prioritize domestic production capacity
- Environmental/regulatory action against supplier facilities

### Timeline & Impact Progression

**Week 0–4: Detection Phase**
- Suppliers notify Argentine importers of order delays
- Hospital pharmacy directors report longer lead times
- ANMAT initiates inquiry into alternative suppliers
- **Patient Impact:** MINIMAL—existing 30–60 day inventory absorbs gap

**Week 5–8: Constraint Phase**
- Second-line suppliers (China, Japan) increase orders; lead times extend to 120+ days
- Price increases 20–35% as competition for limited non-Indian supply intensifies
- Provincial hospital systems begin rationing to high-priority cases (curative intent only)
- **Patient Impact:** MODERATE—treatment delays of 2–3 weeks; some cycle postponements

**Week 9–16: Acute Shortage Phase**
- Hospital inventory drops below 15-day supply threshold
- Private sector outbids public institutions for available stock
- Off-label substitution attempts (carboplatin, oxaliplatin) considered despite contraindication in certain histologies
- **Patient Impact:** HIGH—treatment interruptions; ~30–40% of scheduled cycles delayed >2 weeks

**Week 17+: Crisis Phase**
- National stockout likely if no alternative supplier activated
- Diversion of cisplatin from private clinics to public sector (potential black market)
- Regional collaboration (Chile, Uruguay, Paraguay) for small quantities (insufficient to cover demand)
- **Patient Impact:** SEVERE—treatment abandonment in curative-intent settings; progression of Stage III/IV disease; estimated 300–500 additional cancer deaths within 12 months attributable to treatment delay

**Healthcare System Strain:**

- Private sector profit margin compression: 8–15% (supply shortage limits pricing power; bulk buyers shift to alternatives)

## Policy Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (Weeks 0–4)

1. **Activate ANMAT Emergency Procedure**
   - Expedite approval of alternative suppliers (China: Hospira/Pfizer plants; Japan: Nichi-Iko; Mexico: Laboratorios Liomont)
   - Reduce documentation requirements from 6–8 months to 6–8 weeks for WHO-GMP certified suppliers
   - Allow emergency importation under conditional approval pending full dossier review

2. **Establish Interagency Supply Task Force**
   - Coordinate ANMAT, Ministry of Health, PAMI, and hospital administrators
   - Daily inventory reporting from major provincial centers
   - Prioritize allocation protocol (public hospitals > private sector)
