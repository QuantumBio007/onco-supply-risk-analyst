# Drug Shortage Risk Brief: Cisplatin in Argentina
## API Export Restriction Scenario

---

## Drug Profile

**Drug:** Cisplatin (Cisplatinum)
**Therapeutic Class:** Platinum-based alkylating chemotherapy agent
**Clinical Indications:** Testicular cancer, ovarian cancer, bladder cancer, head & neck cancers, lung cancer
**Formulation in Argentina:** Injectable solution (10 mg/5mL, 50 mg/25mL vials); powder for reconstitution
**Annual Estimated Demand (Argentina):** 8,000–12,000 treatment cycles (~200–300 kg API equivalent)
**Regulatory Status:** ANMAT-approved; included in National Cancer Treatment Program

**Market Structure:**
- Primary suppliers: Pfizer, Hospira (Pfizer subsidiary), Actavis, Sandoz
- Domestic manufacturing: Limited (primarily formulation/packaging; no active API synthesis)
- Import dependency: ~95% of API sourced from India, China, and EU

---

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

### Critical Vulnerabilities

**1. API Source Concentration**
- India represents 40–50% of global cisplatin API exports; primary suppliers: Cipla, Dr. Reddy's, Aurobindo
- China supplies 25–30% (Hubei, Shandong provinces)
- Single-source risk: Argentina lacks redundant supplier agreements
- Lead time from India: 60–90 days (ocean freight)

**2. Domestic Manufacturing Gaps**
- No active API synthesis capacity in Argentina
- Formulation/packaging occurs at 2–3 local sites (Roemmers, Farmapiel, Laboratorio Richet)
- These facilities are contract manufacturers dependent on imported APIs
- No strategic national reserve

**3. Regulatory & Logistical Constraints**
- ANMAT import licensing requires 15–30 days per shipment
- Cold-chain requirements (2–8°C) add complexity; limited refrigerated storage infrastructure in regional hospitals
- Currency volatility in Argentina increases procurement costs by 20–40% during economic downturns

**4. Market Competition**
- High-income OECD countries (US, EU) will prioritize allocation if global scarcity emerges
- Argentina's purchasing power lower than competing markets; risk of deprioritization

---

## Scenario Impact Analysis

### Trigger: API Export Restriction

**Assumption:** India implements temporary (6–12 month) or permanent export quota/licensing restriction on cisplatin API due to:
- Domestic supply constraints
- Regulatory enforcement (environmental compliance at manufacturing sites)
- Geopolitical friction or trade protectionism

---

### Phase 1: Immediate Impact (Weeks 1–4)

| Metric | Impact Level | Details |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| **Supply Disruption** | HIGH | In-transit shipments (60–90 days pipeline) unaffected; new orders blocked |
| **Hospital Stock Depletion** | MODERATE | Most hospitals maintain 4–8 week buffer; critical stockouts unlikely immediately |
| **Price Volatility** | CRITICAL | Ex-India pricing +150–300%; gray-market premiums possible |
| **Treatment Delays** | LOW–MODERATE | Elective/adjuvant cases delayed; urgent cases (testicular) prioritized |

**Estimated Affected Patients:** 400–600 patients/month delayed ≥2 weeks

---

### Phase 2: Mid-Term (Months 2–4)

| Metric | Impact Level | Details |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| **Hospital Stockouts** | MODERATE–HIGH | Regional hospitals (provinces) exhaust reserves first |
| **Rationing** | HIGH | ANMAT may implement allocation protocols; preference for curative indications (testicular, ovarian) over palliative |
| **Treatment Abandonment** | MODERATE | Estimated 10–15% of planned cycles cancelled; patients shift to alternative regimens (carboplatin, taxane-based) |
| **Clinical Outcomes** | MODERATE | Inferior outcomes expected for cisplatin-sensitive tumors; ~5–8% reduction in 5-year survival for testicular cancer if delays >3 weeks |
| **Healthcare Costs** | HIGH | Increased use of salvage therapies; hospitalization for complications |

**Estimated Affected Patients:** 1,200–1,800 cumulative patients experience treatment modification or delay

---

### Phase 3: Extended Impact (Months 5+)

| Metric | Impact Level | Details |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| **Supply Stabilization** | Dependent on policy | Alternative suppliers (China, EU) may partially offset; lead times 90–120 days |
| **Market Reallocation** | HIGH | Argentina loses negotiating power; price premiums may persist 12+ months post-crisis |
| **Clinical Portfolio Shift** | MODERATE | Oncology practice shifts toward carboplatin, combination taxane regimens; departure from gold-standard protocols |

---

### Cascading Risks

1. **Secondary Market Distortion**
   - Carboplatin prices increase 40–60% due to substitution demand
   - Docetaxel, paclitaxel shortages possible
   - Cost burden on healthcare system borne by provinces

2. **Equity Impact**
   - Private/CABA patients access gray-market supplies; public system (INSSJP, provincial hospitals) prioritized for government reserves
   - Regional disparities widen (interior provinces receive 30–40% less allocation)

3. **Regulatory Strain**
   - ANMAT expedited licensing may reduce safety surveillance capability
   - Importation of non-GMP compliant batches possible from intermediary suppliers

## Policy Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (Days 1–30)

**1. Strategic Inventory Audit**
- ANMAT + provincial health ministries conduct 72-hour census of cisplatin reserves
- Establish emergency stockpile threshold: ≥500 kg API (4–6 months supply)
- **Responsible parties:** ANMAT, Ministerio de Salud

**2. Supplier Diversification**
- Issue expedited RFQ to non-Indian suppliers:
  - China (Hubei Gotanda, Shandong Weigao): 30–40 kg/week
  - EU (Germany: Teva; Italy
