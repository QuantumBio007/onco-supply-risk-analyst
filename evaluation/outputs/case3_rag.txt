## Drug Profile

Cisplatin is a platinum-based chemotherapy agent on the WHO Model List of Essential Medicines. It is generic and off-patent globally. Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient (API) manufacturing is concentrated in two countries: India and China. No alternative regional API sources exist for Latin American manufacturers. Generic market price pressure has caused some producers to exit the market, and single-source or dual-source API scenarios for specific formulations are common. Cisplatin experienced documented global supply disruptions in 2023 linked to manufacturing consolidation at Indian API suppliers.

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

**API Supply Concentration**
Any export restriction, regulatory action, or geopolitical disruption in India or China simultaneously affects all generic manufacturers globally. Argentina has no domestic API manufacturing capability and is fully import-dependent.

**Procurement System Fragmentation**
Argentina's oncology drug procurement operates across multiple channels: public hospitals, obras sociales (employer-based insurance), PAMI (pensioner coverage), and private systems. No single entity maintains complete, integrated, real-time operational visibility into national cisplatin stock across all channels. ANMAT registration data allow retrospective traceability but do not provide actionable inventory visibility during emergencies.

**Currency and Payment Risk**
Cisplatin is traded in USD globally. Argentine procurement budgets are denominated in Argentine pesos. Each devaluation episode simultaneously reduces purchasing power across all payers. Suppliers facing delayed payments reduce credit extension to Argentine distributors, compressing inventory levels downstream.

**Access Fragmentation Beyond Coverage**
Coverage by obras sociales or PAMI does not guarantee access: patients without coverage cannot automatically use those channels. When a public hospital stock depletes, patients are not automatically redirected to other payer channels. Treatment interruption and delay are structural risks, not exceptional failure modes.

**Regulatory Pathway Constraints**
If the only registered manufacturer of a specific cisplatin formulation exits the market, the standard supply pathway is interrupted until another manufacturer completes ANMAT registration—a process measured in months to years. Compassionate access mechanisms exist but do not function as replacements for population-level supply.

**Shared API Vulnerability with Carboplatin**
Cisplatin and carboplatin share the same API supply chain. Simultaneous disruption of both drugs is plausible under an export restriction scenario, compounding shortage risk across the platinum-based chemotherapy class.

## Scenario Impact Analysis

**Modeled Outcome Under API Export Restriction**

Monte Carlo simulation (500 runs, 365-day horizon) under API export restriction conditions projects:

- **Stockout days per year:** 7.9 ± 1.35 (95% CI)
- **Unit service level (fill rate):** 98.0% ± 0.0%
- **Days without stockout (operational service level):** 97.8%
- **Probability of any stockout during the year:** 30%
- **Probability of critical shortage (>60 stockout days):** 1.0%
- **Average inventory on hand:** 549 units

**Model Assumptions**
- Mean lead time during disruption: 87.5 days (std: 21.9 days, log-normal distribution)
- Effective order fill rate: 50% (reflects severe global allocation constraints documented in 2023 shortage)
- Disruption duration: ~90 days (geometric distribution)
- Safety stock: 290 units; Reorder point: 990 units; Economic order quantity: 348 units

**Interpretation**
Quantitative projections show cisplatin supply within acceptable operational range (service level 97.8%) even under API export restriction. However, this aggregate measure masks critical vulnerabilities:

1. **Distributed Shortage Risk:** 30% probability of any stockout means shortages are not rare events. Stockout days, though modest on average, may concentrate in specific facilities or patient cohorts.

2. **Visibility Gap Risk:** The 7.9-day average stockout is calculated at a national aggregate level. Argentina has no mechanism to detect and redistribute available stock across fragmented channels in real time. A hospital experiencing stockout may not know whether transferable stock exists elsewhere in the system.

3. **Equity Risk:** Public hospitals and smaller obras sociales, most exposed to currency devaluation and payment delays, are likely to experience shortages before larger, better-capitalized systems. The modeled "7.9 days" represents an average; distribution across payers is unknown.

4. **Clinical Impact Window:** Even 7.9 stockout days/year in oncology is clinically significant. Each day represents delayed dose cycles for patients on active treatment. Modeling does not capture whether interruptions cluster during critical treatment phases.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Establish a National Strategic Cisplatin Reserve**
   - Maintain a 60–90 day buffer stock (1,800–2,700 units) independent of routine procurement to absorb API export disruptions without relying on real-time imports. Store across geographically distributed public hospital hubs to reduce single-point risk.

2. **Create Actionable National Inventory Visibility**
   - Develop an operational-time inventory dashboard linking public hospitals, major obras sociales, and PAMI. The system must enable real-time stock queries and inter-facility loan authorization within hours, not days. Distinguish between regulatory traceability and operational visibility.

3. **Coordinate Cross-Channel Procurement**
   - Establish standing agreements allowing public hospitals to borrow cisplatin from obras sociales and PAMI reserves during acute shortages, with reimbursement protocols. Reduce fragmentation of purchasing power by pooling demand forecasting across channels.

4. **Insulate from Currency Volatility**
   - Negotiate multi-year fixed-price API supply contracts in USD for cisplatin and carboplatin, hedged against peso devaluation. Link procurement budgets to stabilization accounts to prevent simultaneous demand collapse during devaluation episodes.

5. **Accelerate Alternative Manufacturer Registration**
   - ANMAT should fast-track registration for additional cisplatin formulations from different manufacturers to reduce single-source dependency. Maintain a list of pre-qualified manufacturers whose dossiers are review-ready to enable emergency registration within weeks, not months.

6. **Establish Clinical Escalation Protocols**
   - Develop transparent, hospital-wide protocols for treatment modification, dose scheduling adjustments, and inter-facility transfers triggered by shortage alerts. Train oncology teams and social workers in advance; avoid ad-hoc judicialización as the primary shortage response.
