## Drug Profile

Cisplatin is a platinum-based alkylating chemotherapy agent used to treat testicular, ovarian, bladder, lung, and head and neck cancers. **Cisplatin is on the WHO Model List of Essential Medicines (oncology section), where it has been listed since 1979.** It is fully off-patent with multiple generic manufacturers worldwide. **The active pharmaceutical ingredient is manufactured overwhelmingly in India and China, which together account for over 80% of global cisplatin API supply.** Argentina has no domestic API manufacturing capacity and is fully import-dependent. Cisplatin is available as an injectable solution or powder for reconstitution and must be stored at room temperature (15–25°C), protected from light — notably, refrigeration causes precipitation and renders the product unusable.

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

Argentina's cisplatin supply chain faces multiple structural vulnerabilities:

**Concentrated API Origin:** Over 80% of global cisplatin API originates from India and China. Any export restriction, regulatory enforcement action, or geopolitical disruption in either country simultaneously affects all generic manufacturers globally, including those supplying Argentina.

**Generic Market Margins & Manufacturer Exit:** The fully generic status of cisplatin creates intense price competition that reduces manufacturer margins and has historically driven market consolidation. This increases the risk that suppliers will exit the market during periods of low profitability, shrinking the supplier base further.

**Regulatory Clearance Lag:** Argentina's ANMAT (Administración Nacional de Medicamentos, Alimentos y Tecnología Médica) requires approval of any new supplier or alternative manufacturer before legal distribution. Standard ANMAT registration timelines are 12–18 months for generic drugs. During an API export restriction, this regulatory requirement becomes a critical constraint — even if alternative suppliers exist globally, they cannot supply Argentina until ANMAT registration is complete, effectively extending practical lead times by 6–18 months.

**Fragmented Procurement Visibility:** Argentina's multi-channel procurement landscape (public hospitals, obras sociales, PAMI, prepagas, and provincial drug banks) creates structural information gaps. No single entity has real-time integrated visibility into national oncology drug stock across all channels. While regulatory traceability systems exist, they are not equivalent to an operational inventory dashboard. This fragmentation means the health system cannot rapidly identify and reallocate available transferable stock during an emergency.

**Formal Coverage vs. Real Access Gap:** A critical distinction in the Argentine system exists between nominal coverage and actual access. Many oncology treatments are formally covered by obras sociales or the public system, but actual access depends on a downstream chain including clinical authorization, reimbursement file processing, supplier contracts, and physical delivery logistics. Each step introduces latency and failure risk independent of drug availability in aggregate. During a supply shock, this gap compounds scarcity.

**Shared Platinum API Supply Chain with Carboplatin:** Cisplatin and carboplatin share the same API supply chain (India and China). An Indian or Chinese API export restriction, manufacturing shutdown, or quality enforcement action disrupts both drugs simultaneously, eliminating substitution flexibility for oncology programs that use both platinum agents.

**Risk Classification: LOW**

The modeled scenario assumes:
- Effective order fill rate of 50% (structural floor of 90% × scenario constraint of 55%), reflecting severe allocation constraints documented during the 2023–2024 cisplatin/carboplatin shortage
- Effective budget multiplier of 75%, reflecting chronic fiscal constraints in Argentina independent of the scenario shock
- Disruption duration of ~90 days (geometric distribution), modeling recovery dynamics
- Lead times extending to 87.5 days on average during the disruption window

**Interpretation:**

Under an API export restriction, cisplatin supply in Argentina is projected to experience 7.9 stockout days per year, with a unit service level of 98.0%. This result is within an acceptable operational range for routine inventory management. However, this "low risk" classification carries important caveats:

1. **Assumes Budget Stability:** The 75% effective budget multiplier assumes Argentina can sustain procurement spending during the disruption. Fiscal constraints or competing budget pressures could force this multiplier downward, shifting risk to MODERATE or CRITICAL.

2. **Does Not Capture Access Fragmentation Delays:** The simulation models inventory stockouts but does not quantify the additional latency introduced by the multi-channel procurement system, ANMAT regulatory clearance backlogs for alternative suppliers, and formal-vs.-real-access gaps. Actual time-to-patient access during a disruption would exceed the modeled lead time.

3. **Non-Substitutable Drug:** The shared API supply chain with carboplatin means that if an export restriction hits India/China, both cisplatin and carboplatin become scarce simultaneously. Oncology protocols cannot shift to the alternative platinum agent.

4. **Vulnerable Subpopulations:** Uninsured patients without obras sociales or prepaga coverage depend entirely on public hospital supply. If public systems experience stockouts, these patients have no channel fallback and may face treatment delay or suspension. The amparo de salud mechanism exists but is not a substitute for institutional supply reliability.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Establish a Strategic Buffer Stock:** Maintain a national-level strategic reserve of 90–120 days of cisplatin consumption (approximately 1,200–1,600 units based on modeled inventory metrics). Pre-position this stock in public hospital networks and provincial drug banks to enable rapid reallocation during disruption.

2. **Secure Advance Alternative Supplier Registration:** Proactively identify secondary and tertiary cisplatin API suppliers outside India and China (e.g., in Europe or other regions) and initiate ANMAT pre-registration processes during normal supply periods. This compresses the 12–18 month regulatory lag that would otherwise extend lead times during an actual export restriction.

3. **Implement Real-Time Inventory Visibility:** Develop an integrated, near-real-time national cisplatin inventory dashboard spanning public hospitals, obras sociales, PAMI, and provincial systems. Designate a single coordinating authority (e.g., within ANMAT or the Ministry of Health) to access this dashboard and trigger reallocation protocols when any node approaches a pre-set alert threshold.

4. **Formalize Emergency Access Pathways:** Document and standardize the semi-formal mechanisms (inter-hospital loans, emergency purchases outside tender cycles, treatment protocol modifications) currently used to resolve individual shortages. Create a formal emergency procurement protocol that allows public hospitals to access private stock or provincial reserves within 48–72 hours without standard tender delays.

5. **Coordinate Across Payers for Demand Aggregation:** Negotiate formal demand-sharing agreements between PAMI, obras sociales, and public hospital procurement units. During a disruption, aggregate demand forecasting and centralized supplier contact reduce individual channel competition and improve negotiating power with whatever suppliers remain available.

6. **Monitor Regulatory & Geopolitical Triggers:** Establish monthly monitoring of API manufacturing status, regulatory enforcement actions, and export policy signals from India and China. Link supply chain alerts to the Ministry of Health's strategic pharmaceutical planning cycle, with trigger points at 60, 30, and 14 days before projected shortage onset.

7. **Address Carboplatin Simultaneously:** Because cisplatin and carboplatin share the API supply chain, any buffer stock, alternative supplier registration, or inventory visibility initiative for cisplatin must also include carboplatin. Treat both platinum agents as a single supply chain unit.

## Confidence & Limitations

**Confidence:**
- The modeled stockout projections (7.9 days/year, 98.0% service level) are derived from validated continuous-review inventory theory (Warren textbook, Badejo & Ierapetritou disruption dynamics) applied to documented 2023–2024 cisplatin shortage parameters.
- The 87.5-day disruption-period lead time is supported by historical API export delays during 2023–2024 shortage episodes.
- The 50% effective fill rate reflects observed allocation constraints during the 2023–2024 shortage.
- The 75% budget multiplier is conservative and reflects Argentina's documented chronic fiscal austerity (oil revenue collapse, structural spending caps).

**Limitations:**
1. **Simulation Does Not Capture Fragmentation Delays:** The inventory model projects physical stockouts but does not quantify the additional latency from multi-channel procurement fragmentation, ANMAT clearance backlogs for alternative suppliers, or the formal-vs.-real-access gap. Actual patient access delays may exceed modeled lead times by weeks or months.

2. **Budget Assumption Sensitivity:** The forecast is highly sensitive to the 75% budget multiplier assumption. If fiscal constraints force this lower (e.g., to 50%), risk would escalate to MODERATE or CRITICAL. Conversely, if emergency procurement budgets are unlocked, risk would improve.

3. **Carboplatin Dependency Not Separately Quantified:** The brief identifies the shared API supply chain with carboplatin but does not separately model carboplatin shortage risk or quantify the therapeutic impact of simultaneous cisplatin/carboplatin scarcity on treatment protocols.

4. **Regulatory Clearance Timing Uncertain:** The 12–18 month ANMAT registration window is an estimate. Actual timelines depend on application completeness, inspectorate workload, and manufacturing site history. Pre-registration activities may compress this, but the uncertainty remains.

5. **Procurement Data Granularity:** National consumption figures and supplier contracts for cisplatin in Argentina are not fully transparent in public sources. Modeled demand distributions (normal, truncated) are typical for mature generic oncology drugs but reflect general category assumptions, not Argentina-specific disaggregated hospital-level data.

6. **Carboplatin Substitution Assumption:** The brief assumes carboplatin cannot substitute for cisplatin if cisplatin becomes scarce (because both share the API supply chain). However, some clinical scenarios may allow protocol modification. The impact of such substitution is not quantified.

7. **Geopolitical Scenario Definition:** "API export restriction" is modeled
