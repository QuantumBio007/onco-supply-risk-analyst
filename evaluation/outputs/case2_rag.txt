## Drug Profile

Trastuzumab is a monoclonal antibody targeting the HER2 receptor, used to treat HER2-positive breast cancer and HER2-positive gastric cancer. It was originally developed by Genentech (marketed as Herceptin by Roche). **Trastuzumab was added to the WHO Model List of Essential Medicines core list in 2019**, confirming its status as a globally essential medicine.

Unlike small-molecule chemotherapy agents (cisplatin, doxorubicin), trastuzumab is a biologic manufactured through cell culture processes. **There is no India/China generic API dynamic for this drug**—the manufacturing base is concentrated among a small number of specialized biologic facilities globally. Multiple biosimilars are approved internationally (Herzuma, Ogivri, Kanjinti, and others), though biosimilar uptake in Latin America remains slower than in high-income countries due to regulatory complexity and physician familiarity.

Trastuzumab carries a critical cold chain requirement: it must be refrigerated at 2–8°C throughout the entire supply chain from manufacture to patient. Any break in the cold chain compromises product integrity. A full treatment course costs USD 50,000–70,000 per patient per year (originator); even biosimilars are substantially more expensive than generic small-molecule chemotherapy.

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

Venezuela's oncology drug supply chain faces structural collapse. The Lancet Oncology (2017) reported that Venezuela had available only approximately **10% of medicines needed for cancer care**. Trastuzumab is **unavailable in the public sector for most patients throughout 2020–2025** and is accessible only via private dollarized purchase or NGO emergency supply. Approximately 4,000 pediatric oncology patients per year contact NGO networks for medication, with fewer than 10% obtaining oncology drugs through formal channels.

**Key vulnerabilities specific to trastuzumab:**

1. **Cold chain fragility:** Port delays without adequate refrigeration, customs holds, and power outages at warehouses can render entire shipments unusable. This risk is higher in Venezuela than in high-income countries due to infrastructure variability and chronic power outages.

2. **Procurement system collapse:** Venezuela operates at chronic fiscal constraints (oil revenue collapse, structural austerity, ~40% of orders fail or arrive severely short due to lack of foreign currency). As of September 2025, El Nacional reported "massive debt with suppliers and closure of international credit lines" resulting from chronic foreign-currency shortage for sanitary imports.

3. **Cost-driven access barrier:** Trastuzumab's high unit cost (orders of magnitude more expensive than generic chemotherapy) means budget pressure affects access first and most severely. The effective budget multiplier for oncology procurement is approximately 30% of normative needs.

4. **Lead time prolongation:** Mean lead time for trastuzumab imports during stressed conditions is 60 days (standard deviation 36 days), compared to 14–21 days under normal international supply conditions.

5. **Concentrated supplier base:** Unlike cisplatin or doxorubicin, which are small-molecule generics with distributed manufacturing, trastuzumab's biologic manufacturing base is concentrated among a limited number of global producers, reducing alternative sourcing flexibility.

## Scenario Impact Analysis

**Baseline (Normal Operations) Scenario:**

Under baseline conditions with structural Venezuelan constraints (57% effective order fill rate, 30% effective budget constraint, 60-day mean lead time), the Monte Carlo simulation (500 runs) projects:

- **Stockout days per year: 79.3 days (95% CI: ±1.2 days)**
- **Unit service level: 73.7%**
- **Probability of any stockout during the year: 100%**
- **Probability of critical shortage (>60 stockout days): 91.2%**
- **Average inventory on hand: 4 units**

This represents a **CRITICAL supply disruption risk** that directly threatens treatment continuity for cancer patients.

**Interpretation:** Even under "normal" (baseline) conditions, the modeled projections indicate that trastuzumab will be unavailable for approximately 22% of the year (79 days). This baseline itself reflects chronic structural procurement failures in Venezuela — not a temporary shock scenario. The 57% fill rate and 30% budget multiplier are structural parameters, not scenario-specific; they represent the persistent fiscal and logistical constraints documented in the Venezuelan health system as of 2025.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Declare trastuzumab a critical essential medicine requiring emergency procurement status:** Given WHO EML core-list status (2019), persistent Venezuela shortages constitute a failure of the health system's obligation to provide essential cancer care. Establish a dedicated foreign-currency allocation for trastuzumab independent of general pharmaceutical procurement constraints.

2. **Establish a strategic cold-chain reserve:** Procure and maintain a 90-unit safety stock (sufficient for approximately 30 days of supply at baseline demand) stored under controlled refrigeration with backup power. This aligns with the modeled reorder point of 180 units and safety stock calculation.

3. **Diversify biosimilar sourcing:** Accelerate regulatory and procurement pathways for approved trastuzumab biosimilars (Herzuma, Ogivri, Kanjinti) to reduce dependence on single-source originator supply and expand competition on price and availability.

4. **Develop NGO supply-chain coordination:** Formalize data-sharing agreements with NGO networks currently providing emergency supply (~10% of oncology patients). These networks possess real-time visibility into access gaps that formal health ministry procurement systems do not capture.

5. **Establish cold-chain infrastructure audits:** Conduct mandatory certification of all warehouses and distribution centers handling trastuzumab to prevent product loss due to refrigeration failure. Power backup (UPS, generators) should be mandatory in Venezuela's high-outage environment.

6. **Implement WHO shortage reporting:** Report baseline trastuzumab unavailability to the WHO medicines shortage reporting platform. EML inclusion creates normative reporting obligations; systematic documentation will trigger international attention and potential donor support.

## Confidence & Limitations

**Confidence:**

- The baseline projection (79.3 stockout days/year) is derived from a validated continuous-review inventory model (Warren OM framework) with 500 Monte Carlo simulations, providing robust quantification of supply disruption under stated assumptions.
- Venezuela-specific procurement parameters (57% fill rate, 30% budget constraint, 60-day lead time) are grounded in documented sources: Duma & Duque Duran (Journal of Global Oncology 2019); Convite March 2024 monitoring bulletin; El Nacional September 2025 reporting.
- WHO EML status (core list, 2019) is definitively established.

**Limitations:**

- **Cold chain failure rate not modeled:** The simulation assumes that ordered trastuzumab arrives refrigerated; it does not quantify the probability that shipments are damaged in transit or at customs due to refrigeration failure. The 57% fill rate is a structural procurement constraint, not a product-loss parameter.
- **NGO supply chain not captured:** Approximately 10% of pediatric oncology patients access drugs through NGO networks rather than formal procurement. The baseline simulation models only formal health sector supply, potentially underestimating de-facto availability in private and NGO channels.
- **Demand estimation uncertainty:** The simulation uses Poisson demand distribution with parameter λ inferred from historical use, but historical data may not reflect suppressed demand (patients unable to access trastuzumab). Actual demand under improved supply would likely be higher.
- **Biosimilar uptake dynamics not modeled:** The brief does not quantify how rapid adoption of lower-cost biosimilars might expand effective budget capacity or reduce per-unit procurement pressure.
- **Recovery dynamics in disruption scenarios:** The baseline scenario is framed as "normal operations," but the structural parameters (57% fill rate, 30% budget) already represent a persistently disrupted state. The model does not distinguish between steady-state shortage and acute shock recovery.
- **Regulatory pathway delays:** Venezuela's capacity to approve and register biosimilars for accelerated access is not documented in the retrieved context; this was inferred from regional patterns in Colombia and Argentina.
