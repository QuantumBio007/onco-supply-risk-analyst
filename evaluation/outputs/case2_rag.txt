## Drug Profile

Trastuzumab is a HER2-targeted monoclonal antibody (biologic) indicated for HER2-positive breast and gastric cancers. It is on the WHO Model List of Essential Medicines. The drug is patent-protected (originator: Herceptin, USD 50,000–70,000 per patient per year) with biosimilar alternatives available at lower but still substantial cost. Trastuzumab requires continuous cold-chain maintenance (2–8°C) throughout the entire supply chain from manufacture to patient administration. Any break in refrigeration renders the product unusable.

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

Venezuela is 100% import-dependent for trastuzumab; there is no domestic manufacturing capacity. The supply chain faces multiple structural barriers unrelated to scenario shocks:

**Procurement system collapse:** The public procurement system (MPPS/INHRR) operates far below meaningful coverage. Budget allocation to healthcare fell from 8.1% of GDP (2006) to 1.18% (2017). Only approximately 30% of total health spending is publicly financed; 62.99% is out-of-pocket (compared to regional average of ~28%). Trastuzumab access is limited to: private dollarized purchase (accessible only to a small population fraction), international NGO donations, and degraded IVSS (social security) channels.

**Cold-chain infrastructure failure:** 66% of Venezuela's largest hospitals lack consistent running water; analogous constraints apply to refrigeration. Power instability is documented and widespread, creating chronic risk of shipment degradation during storage and transit.

**Regulatory and currency barriers:** INHRR drug registration requires 12–18+ months, creating baseline lead-time delays. Import approvals have historically been tied to foreign-exchange allocations controlled by the government. Venezuela's currency control systems have systematically restricted hard-currency access for pharmaceutical importers—a permanent structural barrier, not a scenario-specific shock.

**Order fulfillment rate:** Chronic fiscal constraints and informal supply chains result in approximately 40% of orders failing or arriving severely short. Modeled effective order fill rate: 57%.

## Scenario Impact Analysis

**Baseline (Normal Operations) Simulation Results:**
- **Stockout days per year:** 117.3 ± 1.35 days (95% CI)
- **Unit service level (fill rate):** 73.2% ± 0.0%
- **Days without stockout:** 67.9%
- **Average inventory on hand:** 3 units
- **Probability of any stockout during the year:** 100%
- **Probability of critical shortage (>60 stockout days):** 99.8%

**Risk Assessment:** CRITICAL

The baseline scenario projects trastuzumab supply in Venezuela to experience 117.3 stockout days annually—approximately one-third of the year without reliable access. This reflects chronic structural procurement failures and the permanent ceiling on effective budget allocation (30% of nominal needs). 

The context explicitly states that Venezuela's baseline is "effectively a sustained crisis," and additional scenario shocks (API export restriction, currency devaluation) produce only marginal increases in stockout risk because "the baseline is already near the floor." The baseline Venezuelan stockout projection (117.3 days) is substantially higher in absolute terms than Argentina's baseline (5.1 days), reflecting the structural collapse of Venezuela's health system rather than temporary disruption.

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Establish dedicated NGO/international donation coordination:** Given that public procurement cannot reliably supply trastuzumab, formalize pathways for international NGO and donation programs to ensure predictable access for HER2+ cancer patients. Document patient registries to prioritize high-need populations.

2. **Create emergency cold-chain redundancy:** Invest in solar-powered or battery-backed refrigeration units at key hospital nodes to mitigate power outages and preserve shipments. Prioritize facilities serving the highest volume of HER2+ patients.

3. **Streamline INHRR registration for trastuzumab biosimilars:** Reduce registration timelines below the current 12–18 months to enable faster market entry of lower-cost alternatives and increase supply chain competition.

4. **Establish a bilateral procurement agreement with a neighboring country (Colombia):** Explore cross-border agreements or emergency access protocols to allow Venezuelan patients to access trastuzumab through regional supply chains during domestic shortages.

5. **Ring-fence hard currency for oncology biologics:** Advocate for government allocation of foreign exchange specifically for essential biologics (trastuzumab, other HER2 agents) as a public health priority, separate from general pharmaceutical procurement.

## Confidence & Limitations

**Confidence:**
- The structural description of Venezuela's health system collapse, budget constraints, and import dependence is grounded in cited sources (Ballard Brief, HRW, Convite, IHP UK).
- Cold-chain vulnerability and refrigeration infrastructure failures are documented.
- The Monte Carlo simulation follows standard operations management methodology (Warren textbook, continuous-review inventory model).

**Limitations:**
- The baseline stockout projection (117.3 days) is derived from a simplified inventory model with assumed parameters (57% order fill rate, 30% effective budget multiplier, 60-day mean lead time). These are illustrative estimates, not actuarial data from Venezuelan health system records.
- No primary procurement data from MPPS or IVSS was available; parameters reflect structural conditions reported in secondary sources.
- The simulation assumes no recovery or improvement in disruption duration ("worst-case bound"); actual dynamics may vary.
- Donation and NGO supply flows are not quantified in the model; actual stockout exposure may be partially mitigated by these channels, though visibility into their reliability is limited.
- Patient-level access outcomes (who receives treatment, clinical outcomes) are not modeled; the brief addresses supply-chain risk only, not end-to-end healthcare delivery equity.
- The model does not capture informal private supply chains or pricing dynamics for dollarized out-of-pocket access.
