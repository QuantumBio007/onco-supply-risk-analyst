## Drug Profile

Carboplatin is a second-generation platinum-based chemotherapy agent on the WHO Model List of Essential Medicines (oncology section). It is used to treat ovarian, lung, head and neck, and other cancers. The drug is fully off-patent with multiple generic manufacturers globally. Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient (API) manufacturing is concentrated in India and China, mirroring the cisplatin supply chain. Carboplatin is formulated as an injectable solution and requires only room-temperature storage (15–30°C), without cold-chain requirements.

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

Venezuela's carboplatin supply chain operates within a structurally collapsed procurement system, not a strained but functional one. The baseline operating environment includes:

- **Import dependency at 100%**: Carboplatin is not manufactured domestically and all supply depends on USD-denominated imports.
- **Currency control barriers**: Hard-currency access for pharmaceutical imports is controlled by the national government through successive currency systems (CADIVI, CENCOEX, DICOM). This creates a permanent structural barrier independent of scenario shocks.
- **Regulatory lead-time risk**: INHRR (Instituto Nacional de Higiene "Rafael Rangel") registration timelines are reported at 12–18 months or longer. Registrations are country-specific and do not transfer from ANMAT (Argentina) or INVIMA (Colombia).
- **Shared API vulnerability**: Carboplatin API supply is concentrated in India and China. An API export restriction from either country would simultaneously disrupt both carboplatin and cisplatin, compounding oncology program impacts.
- **Procurement system collapse**: Healthcare spending fell from 8.1% of GDP (2006) to 1.18% (2017). By August 2023, 72% of the population reported inability to access public health services when needed. The MPPS and IVSS function only as residual structures.
- **Out-of-pocket access dominance**: Approximately 62.99% of total health spending is out-of-pocket (vs. 28% regional average), meaning carboplatin access is limited to private, dollarized purchase (affordable to a small fraction of the population), NGO donation programs, or a severely degraded social security system.

## Scenario Impact Analysis

**Combined Shock Definition**: Simultaneous API export restriction (India/China) + currency devaluation.

**Modeled Outcomes** (500 Monte Carlo simulations, 365-day horizon):
- **Stockout days per year**: 35.2 ± 2.53 days (95% CI)
- **Service level (unit fill rate)**: 90.8% ± 1.0%

- **Probability of any stockout during year**: 100%

- **Average inventory on hand**: 504 units
- **Overall supply risk classification**: **HIGH**

**Key Parameters**:
- Mean lead time during disruption: 150.0 days (std: 90.0 days, log-normal)
- Effective order fill rate: 33% (structural floor 60% × scenario 55%)
- Effective budget multiplier: 18% (structural cap 30% × scenario 60%)
- Disruption duration: ~120 days (geometric distribution)

**Interpretation**: The combined shock produces 35.2 stockout days/year. However, the context notes that this represents only a **marginal increase** from the baseline of 31 stockout days/year because Venezuela's baseline is already at or near crisis floor. The system cannot meaningfully degrade further; treatment delays and rationing occur under both baseline and shock scenarios. The distinction is that combined shocks extend the duration and intensity of an already chronic shortage state rather than creating an acute transition.

## Policy Recommendations

**Note**: Policy recommendations must operate within the constraint that Venezuela's public procurement system is non-functional as an institutional channel. Any intervention below the national level will have limited reach.

1. **International NGO coordination and donation tracking**: Since oncology drug access in Venezuela is primarily mediated through NGO programs and private out-of-pocket purchase, establish transparent inventory tracking and allocation protocols across major NGO suppliers (MSF, direct donation programs) to prevent stock hoarding and optimize coverage for uninsured populations.

2. **Regional procurement cooperation**: Explore negotiated access to carboplatin purchased through Colombia's EPS system or regional pooled procurement mechanisms (PAHO, LAFTA agreements) to leverage Colombia's more stable peso currency and functioning INVIMA registration pathway, conditional on Venezuelan government currency allocation for import payment.

3. **INHRR registration acceleration**: If currency access can be secured, reduce INHRR registration timelines from 12–18 months to <6 months through: (a) pre-submission technical support for registrants, (b) parallel review pathways, or (c) recognition of INVIMA/ANMAT approvals as partial de facto evidence for safety/efficacy to shorten local review scope.

4. **Strategic buffer stock (if currency allocation improves)**: Establish a 45–60 day buffer stock at IVSS distribution hub(s), sized to the optimal safety stock of 1022 units, conditional on securing hard-currency budget allocation.

5. **Admission and allocation protocols**: In the absence of sufficient stock, establish transparent clinical triage protocols for carboplatin allocation to oncology services, with priority to curative-intent regimens (ovarian, testicular cancers) over palliative settings, to maximize clinical benefit under scarcity.

## Confidence & Limitations

**Confidence Level**: MODERATE with substantial caveats.

**Limitations**:

1. **Structural baseline uncertainty**: The simulation assumes a baseline of 31 stockout days/year under non-shock conditions. This figure is derived from publicly available sources (Ballard Brief, BYU, 2023; HRW 2024) but is not independently validated against internal Venezuelan MPPS or INHRR procurement records, which are not publicly accessible.

2. **API restriction assumption**: The combined shock model assumes a 150-day mean lead time during disruption. This is plausible for India/China API export restrictions but is not calibrated to a specific geopolitical scenario or documented policy announcement. Lead time variance under disruption (std: 90 days) reflects high uncertainty.

3. **Fill rate and budget multiplier**: The effective order fill rate of 33% and budget multiplier of 18% are derived by applying scenario factors (55% of structural capacity) to structural parameters
