# Drug Shortage Risk Brief: Carboplatin in Venezuela — Combined Shock Scenario

## Drug Profile

Carboplatin is a second-generation platinum-based chemotherapy agent indicated for ovarian, lung, head and neck, and other solid tumors. It is **included on the WHO Model List of Essential Medicines** (oncology section), reflecting its critical role in cancer treatment globally.

Carboplatin is fully off-patent with multiple generic manufacturers. Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient (API) manufacturing is concentrated in **India and China**. The drug is available as an injectable solution and does not require refrigeration (room temperature storage: 15–30°C, protected from light).

**Critical shared-supply-chain vulnerability:** Carboplatin and cisplatin share the same API supply chain. An API export restriction, manufacturing shutdown, or quality enforcement action in India or China simultaneously disrupts **both cisplatin and carboplatin**, eliminating therapeutic substitution flexibility for oncology programs that use both platinum agents.

## Supply Chain Vulnerability

### Structural Procurement Constraints

In Venezuela, the formal procurement channel is severely constrained:
- The MPPS (Ministerio del Poder Popular para la Salud) nominally directs national drug policy and procurement for oncology drugs.
- The INHRR (Instituto Nacional de Higiene Rafael Rangel) is the regulatory authority; MPPS issues import permits once INHRR grants authorization.
- **In practice, the formal MPPS-INHRR procurement channel is severely constrained by foreign-currency shortages and debt with international suppliers.**
- Effective carboplatin access in Venezuela relies primarily on NGO programs, diaspora procurement, and private dollarized purchase — MPPS formal channels function only residually.

### API Supply Concentration Risk

- Carboplatin API supply originates exclusively from India and China.
- Generic market consolidation reduces manufacturer margins, increasing producer exit risk.
- An Indian or Chinese API export restriction simultaneously affects all global generic manufacturers, including those supplying Latin America.

### Baseline Supply Stress

Even under normal operations (not disrupted), carboplatin in Venezuela experiences:
- **33.1 ± 1.13 stockout days per year** (95% CI)
- Unit fill rate of 91.4%
- Structural order fill rate of only 57% (reflecting chronic procurement failures)
- Average inventory on hand: 114 units

This baseline already indicates **HIGH supply risk** without external shocks.

## Scenario Impact Analysis

### Combined Shock Definition
The combined shock scenario models simultaneous occurrence of:
1. **API export restriction** from India or China (prime supply source)
2. **Currency devaluation** reducing Venezuela's purchasing power for hard-currency imports

### Modeled Outcomes (500 Monte Carlo simulations, 365 days)

| Metric | Value | Interpretation |
|--------|-------|-----------------|
| **Stockout days per year** | 35.2 ± 2.53 (95% CI) | ~1 month of treatment unavailability |
| **Service level (unit fill rate)** | 90.8% ± 1.0% | ~9% of demand unmet |
| **Service level (days without stockout)** | 90.4% | Only 90 days/year without shortage |
| **Probability of any stockout** | 100% | Certainty of shortage occurrence |
| **Probability of critical shortage (>60 days/year)** | 15.8% | High tail risk |
| **CVaR₉₀ (worst-10% mean stockout days)** | 103.7 days | In worst-case runs, >3 months stockout |

### Mechanism of Impact

Under combined shock conditions:
- **Mean lead time extends to 150 days** (std: 90 days), up from 60 days at baseline, due to API supply disruption and supplier prioritization of markets with stronger currencies.
- **Effective order fill rate collapses to 33%** (structural floor 60% × scenario reduction to 55%), compounded by currency constraints.
- **Effective budget multiplier drops to 18%** (structural cap 30% × scenario-driven devaluation to 60%), severely limiting purchasing power.
- **Disruption exposure: ~121.6 days/year** (modeled as ~120-day shock with geometric recovery tail).

**The 35.2 stockout days represents an **additional 2.1-day increase** over baseline (33.1 days), but with higher tail risk and longer individual shortage episodes.**

### Clinical Consequence
Treatment delays and oncology program rationing are likely without intervention. Given carboplatin's role in platinum-based regimens for solid tumors (ovarian, lung, head-and-neck cancers), prolonged unavailability forces:
- Substitution with cisplatin (if available; but same API supply, so likely also in shortage)
- Deferral of chemotherapy cycles
- Treatment regimen modification or abandonment

## Policy Recommendations

**Note:** The recommendations below are based on supply chain structure and modeled risk. They do not constitute clinical guidance on drug substitution or treatment protocols.

1. **Emergency Strategic Reserve:** Establish a 60–90-day buffer stock of carboplatin (estimated 1,800–2,700 units, assuming ~20–30 vials/day national demand) under MPPS custody, protected from currency pressure by hard-currency earmarking or direct import commitment.

2. **Diversify API Sourcing (medium-term):** Advocate through regional health bodies (UNASUR, PAHO) and bilateral channels for second-source API approval from alternate suppliers (e.g., secondary Chinese manufacturers, exploratory European or Japanese suppliers) to reduce India-China concentration risk.

3. **Formal Procurement vs. NGO Coordination:** Formalize a coordination mechanism between MPPS official channels and NGO programs (currently providing ~60–70% of effective supply). Establish memorandum of understanding to avoid duplication and ensure transparent demand accounting.

4. **Hard-Currency Procurement Protocols:** Designate carboplatin and cisplatin as "oncology critical" commodities eligible for preferential foreign-currency allocation during combined shock scenarios, with pre-authorized import authority and fast-tracked INHRR permits.

5. **Shared Platinum-Drug Contingency Planning:** Given the shared API supply chain with cisplatin, coordinate contingency procurement timing for both agents. Do not assume substitutability; plan for simultaneous shortage of both platinum agents.

6. **Regional Supply Visibility:** Participate in PAHO/WHO regional drug shortage alert systems and establish bilateral early-warning agreements with Colombia and Argentina to detect supply chain stress before it reaches crisis threshold.

## Confidence & Limitations

### Data Confidence
- **Modeled outcomes (stockout days, service levels) are based on:**
  - Continuous-review inventory model (Warren Operations Management Textbook §6.6)
  - 500 Monte Carlo simulations with stochastic lead times (log-normal distribution) and geometric disruption duration (Badejo & Ierapetritou 2022)
  - Scenario parameters (lead time 150 days, fill rate 33%, budget multiplier 18%) derived from Venezuela's structural procurement constraints and documented foreign-currency shortages

- **The 35.2 stockout days ± 2.53 figure represents the model's point estimate** with 95% confidence interval. Actual outcomes will depend on implementation of emergency procurement, NGO contributions, and political decisions not captured in the model.

### Limitations
- **Demand distribution:** Modeled as truncated normal; actual oncology demand may exhibit nonlinear behavior (treatment deferral feedback, population migration) not reflected here.
- **NGO supply contribution:** The 57% baseline fill rate assumption reflects chronic MPPS constraints but does not separately model NGO inflow; actual access may be higher or lower depending on diaspora engagement and NGO capacity.
- **Regulatory clearance not modeled:** The model assumes API availability translates to formulation distribution. Actual INHRR permit processing and import authorization delays are not quantified.
- **Substitution dynamics not modeled:** The model treats carboplatin demand as independent; in reality, some patients may shift to cisplatin (if available) or nonplatinum regimens, reducing net carboplatin demand.
- **Currency devaluation rate:** The scenario assumes a 40% devaluation (budget multiplier 60%); actual devaluation may be more or less severe.
- **Comparator countries:** Argentina and Colombia show **MODERATE supply risk** (12.7–17.7 stockout days/year) under the same combined shock, reflecting stronger currency, better regulatory infrastructure, and lower baseline procurement stress. Venezuela's **HIGH risk** reflects structural fragility of the MPPS channel, not pharmaceutical market differences.

### Honest Uncertainty
The model cannot predict:
- Whether a real API export restriction will occur or its duration
- The effectiveness of NGO procurement scaling in response to emergency
- Policy responses (e.g., hard-currency allocation decisions)
- Clinical substitution patterns and treatment deferral rates

**The brief should inform contingency planning, not replace real-time market surveillance and decision-making during an actual supply disruption.**
