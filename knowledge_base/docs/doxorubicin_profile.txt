Doxorubicin is an anthracycline antibiotic chemotherapy agent used to treat breast cancer, leukemia, lymphoma, and sarcomas. It is one of the most broadly used oncology drugs and is a cornerstone of multiple standard chemotherapy regimens.

WHO Essential Medicines status: Doxorubicin is on the WHO Model List of Essential Medicines (oncology section). It exists in two formulations: conventional doxorubicin hydrochloride and liposomal doxorubicin. The conventional form is on the EML; liposomal is used in specific indications.

Patent and generic status: Conventional doxorubicin is fully off-patent with multiple generic manufacturers. Liposomal doxorubicin has fewer manufacturers and higher shortage risk.

Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient (API) origin: India is the dominant global supplier of doxorubicin API, with a smaller contribution from China. Latin American countries have no domestic API manufacturing capacity and are fully import-dependent.

Formulation and storage: Doxorubicin is available as injectable solution and powder for reconstitution. It requires refrigeration at 2–8°C and must be protected from light. The cold chain requirement means that logistics disruptions — port delays, customs holds, power outages — directly threaten product integrity in addition to supply availability.

Currency devaluation impact: Doxorubicin is priced in USD in international trade. In Argentina, hospital procurement budgets are denominated in Argentine pesos. A currency devaluation directly reduces the purchasing power of hospitals and obras sociales buying imported doxorubicin, leading to reduced order volumes, deferred purchases, and increased stockout risk. This dynamic is particularly acute in public hospitals with fixed peso budgets.

Shortage history: Doxorubicin has experienced multiple documented global shortages. In 2011–2012, widespread shortages were reported in the United States due to manufacturing quality issues at major producers. Subsequent shortages occurred in 2022–2023, again linked to API supply concentration and manufacturing consolidation. These global shortages cascade into Latin American markets because the same API suppliers serve both markets.
