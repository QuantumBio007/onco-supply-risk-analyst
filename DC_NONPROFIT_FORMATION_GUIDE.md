# DC Nonprofit Formation Guide — OncoSupply

**Jurisdiction:** Washington, DC  
**Legal Structure:** 501(c)(3) Nonprofit Corporation  
**Timeline:** 60–90 days (fast-track incorporation)  
**Cost Estimate:** $3,500–$5,500 (legal + filing + startup)

### 1.2 Draft Mission Statement

**Active mission (revised 2026-05-03):**

_JCNB Biotech is a nonprofit dedicated to preventing oncology drug shortages in Latin America through AI-driven, multi-dimensional supply chain visibility — combining real-time news intelligence, peer-reviewed Monte Carlo simulation, and institutional knowledge across Argentina, Colombia, and Venezuela. We give ministries, hospitals, and pharmaceutical partners the foresight to act months before patients are harmed._

**Why the rewrite (2026-05-03 strategic review):**
- Original mission claimed "US and Latin America" scope; the codebase, knowledge base, simulation parameters, and field-validation contacts are 100% LATAM. The US scope was aspirational, not operational.
- Angels for Change (US-based, USP/Vizient predictive model for 17 pediatric oncology drugs) already occupies the US oncology shortage prediction niche with institutional backing JCNB cannot match.
- LATAM-deep positioning is defensible (Pablo Castello / Carolina-Souza field network, EPS-IPS debt cascade institutional knowledge, Venezuela structural failure modeling) — the US analogue is not.
- "Multi-dimensional visibility" frames the Phase 2 8-shock-category architecture as the differentiator, not a feature.
- See [phase2_realtime/STRATEGIC_REVIEW_2026-05-03.md](phase2_realtime/STRATEGIC_REVIEW_2026-05-03.md) for full rationale.

**Legacy mission (deprecated 2026-05-03 — kept for audit trail):**

## Phase 2: Legal Formation (Week 2–3)

### 2.1 Incorporate in DC
- [ ] File Articles of Incorporation with DC DCRA
- [ ] Select rush processing (+$100, same-day filing)
- [ ] Filing fee: $250 total
- [ ] Receive Certificate of Incorporation

### 2.2 Obtain EIN (Employer Identification Number)
- [ ] File IRS Form SS-4 (online via IRS.gov)
- [ ] Processing: Instant online
- [ ] Cost: Free
- [ ] Save EIN for all applications

### 2.3 Hire Nonprofit Attorney (Optional but Recommended)
- [ ] Interview 3–5 DC-based nonprofit attorneys
- [ ] Scope: Bylaws, COI policy, 1023 review
- [ ] Cost: $2,000–$3,500
- [ ] Timeline: 2–4 weeks

## Phase 3: Governance Setup (Week 3–4)

### 3.1 Recruit Board of Directors

**Minimum:** 3 members | **Recommended:** 5 members

**Roles needed:**
- Board Chair (strategic/policy background)
- Treasurer (finance/accounting)
- Secretary (legal/compliance)
- 2x Members-at-Large (science/international dev)

- [ ] Identify candidates
- [ ] Conduct interviews (4–6 hrs/month commitment)
- [ ] Obtain COI disclosures
- [ ] Board vote to appoint

### 3.2 Adopt Bylaws
- [ ] Use DC nonprofit default bylaws (attorney or DCRA template)
- [ ] Customize: voting structure, board size, committees, amendments
- [ ] Board approval (formal vote required)
- [ ] Maintain copy in records

### 3.3 Conflict-of-Interest Policy
- [ ] Adopt written COI policy (IRS requirement)
- [ ] Template: councilofnonprofits.org
- [ ] Annual board sign-off (required)

## Phase 4: 501(c)(3) Tax-Exempt Status (Week 5–8)

### 4.1 File IRS Form 1023-EZ (if eligible)
**Use if:** Expected gross receipts < $50K Year 1

- [ ] Complete Form 1023-EZ (2 pages, 15 min)
- [ ] File via IRS e-Services (online)
- [ ] Fee: $275
- [ ] Processing: 2–4 weeks (IRS determines within 24 hours)

### 4.2 Alternative: File IRS Form 1023 (Comprehensive)
**Use if:** Expected gross receipts > $50K OR need stronger defense

- [ ] Complete Form 1023 (15 pages + attachments)
- [ ] Attachments: Bylaws, COI Policy, 3-year financials, narratives, resumes
- [ ] Fee: $600
- [ ] Processing: 2–4 weeks (up to 6 months if issues)

**Recommendation:** Use 1023-EZ for speed; upgrade to 1023 once funded.

### 4.3 IRS 501(c)(3) Determination Letter
- [ ] Receive official letter (keeps letter number on file)
- [ ] Use for: Grant applications, donor tax deductions, state registration
- [ ] Post on website (credibility + transparency)

---

## Phase 5: State & Local Compliance (Week 8–10)

### 5.1 DC Charitable Registration
- [ ] Register with DC Attorney General Charitable Division
- [ ] Form: DC Charitable Organization Registration
- [ ] Required docs: 501(c)(3) Determination Letter, Articles, Form 990
- [ ] Fee: $150
- [ ] Processing: 2–4 weeks

### 5.2 Open Business Bank Account
- [ ] Choose nonprofit-friendly bank (Amalgamated, Bethex, credit union)
- [ ] Required docs:
  - EIN confirmation letter
  - Articles of Incorporation
  - Bylaws
  - ID (Founder/Treasurer)
- [ ] Set up 2–3 authorized signers
- [ ] No minimum balance (nonprofit rates)

### 5.3 Payroll Setup (if hiring staff)
- [ ] Register with DC Department of Employment Services (DOES)
- [ ] Enroll in DC unemployment insurance
- [ ] Set up FICA/tax withholding

---

## Phase 6: Operations & Ongoing Compliance

### 6.1 Annual Form 990 Filing
- [ ] Form 990-N (e-postcard): < $50K gross receipts (free, online)
- [ ] Form 990-NR: > $50K gross receipts ($275 filing fee)
- [ ] Due date: May 15 annually

### 6.2 Annual Board Meetings
- [ ] Schedule annual meeting (Jan–Mar window)
- [ ] Document resolutions: budget approval, officer elections, conflicts update
- [ ] Maintain minute book (legal record)

### 6.3 Nonprofit Insurance
- [ ] General Liability + D&O: $1M–$2M coverage
- [ ] Cost: $1,500–$3,000/year
- [ ] Providers: nonprofitinsurance.org, CHUBB, AIG
